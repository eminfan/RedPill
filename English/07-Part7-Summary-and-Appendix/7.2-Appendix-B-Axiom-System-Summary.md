# Appendix B: Axiom System Summary

## I. Meta-Axioms (Universal and Life Foundational Laws)

### Axiom 1: Entropy Increase Background Axiom
**The universe as a whole tends toward disorder; life, as an ordered structure, must continuously acquire resources from outside to maintain survival—this is the foundation of all life behavior.**
- **Physical Basis**: Second Law of Thermodynamics
- **Social Significance**: Social systems as dissipative structures require continuous resource supply

### Axiom 2: Resource Space-Time Limitation Axiom
**Within a specific time and space, the natural resources directly utilizable by humans have an upper limit; technology can continuously expand resource boundaries but cannot break through the instantaneous space-time limit.**
- **Economic Significance**: Resource scarcity is the fundamental driver of social distribution games
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Resource competition is the core driver of group evolution

### Axiom 3: Group Evolution Priority Axiom
**The core unit of life evolution is the gene group (sharing blood foundations and cultural rules); the long-term survival and security of the group takes priority over individual short-term interests.**
- **Biological Basis**: Group selection theory
- **Social Significance**: The ultimate driver of individual behavior is gene group survival

### Axiom 4: Dual Inheritance System Axiom
**Humans rely on two systems for inheritance: genes determine physiological instincts (very slow evolution); memes (institutions, technology, morality, concepts) determine social rules (very fast evolution), and memes directly determine group fate.**
- **Biological Significance**: Gene-meme co-evolution
- **Social Significance**: Cultural memes are the operating system of social systems

## II. Social Evolution Axioms (Laws Specific to Human Society)

### Axiom 5: Collaborative Value-Addition Axiom
**Through division of labor, collaboration, and innovation, humans can create incremental resources far exceeding the simple sum of individuals; society is not only about competing for existing stock.**
- **Economic Significance**: Division of labor and collaboration improve production efficiency
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Collaboration is the source of group advantage

### Axiom 6: Predatory Profit-Seeking Axiom
**Under spontaneous order without global constraints, some individuals tend to use monopolized resources and controlled power to uncompensatedly appropriate others' labor results, because predation costs less than direct production.**
- **Behavioral Basis**: Rational profit-seeking principle
- **Social Significance**: Predation is the inevitable tendency of spontaneous order

### Axiom 7: Meme Legitimization Axiom
**Groups holding rule-making power will use morality, institutions, and ideology to package their predatory behavior as reasonable, inevitable, and legitimate rules, reducing the costs of governance and stability maintenance.**
- **Sociological Significance**: The social control function of ideology
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Meme domestication is a necessary mechanism for system stability

## III. Social Trophic Level Theory Axioms

### Axiom 8: Dissipative Structure Survival Axiom
**Human society is a typical dissipative system that must continuously acquire survival and development resources from outside, offsetting the system's internal spontaneous disordering trend through resource consumption.**
- **Physical Basis**: Second Law of Thermodynamics
- **Social Significance**: Resource acquisition and efficient utilization are the first prerequisites for social survival

### Axiom 9: Resource Scarcity Axiom
**Within a closed system of a specific space-time, the total amount of survival and development resources available to society has a rigid upper limit.**
- **Economic Basis**: Resource scarcity is a basic assumption of economics
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Resource competition is the fundamental driver of social stratification

### Axiom 10: Rational Profit-Seeking Axiom
**Without global rigid institutional constraints, individuals/groups with resource and power advantages will preferentially choose the behavioral pattern of "lowest cost for maximum benefit."**
- **Behavioral Basis**: Rational agent assumption
- **Social Significance**: Predation is the inevitable tendency of spontaneous order

### Axiom 11: Surplus Resource Natural Enrichment Axiom
**The sole original source of social survival and development resources is the labor creation of bottom-layer production groups; in the spontaneous state without external rigid constraints, surplus resources naturally have a structural tendency to flow from the "production end" to the "rule-making end."**
- **Economic Basis**: Surplus value theory
- **Social Significance**: The directionality of resource flow is the direct cause of social stratification

## IV. Gene Group Evolution Theory Axioms

### Axiom 12: Dual Encoding Essence Axiom
**Humans are life continuation systems jointly constituted and co-evolved by genes and cultural memes.**
- **Gene Layer**: Sets foundational biological instincts (five desires), providing the system's energy supply and collaboration prototype
- **Meme Layer**: Constructs super-biological adaptation systems (rules, institutions, culture), expanding survival strategy space
- **Co-evolutionary Relationship**: Genes provide the material foundation for meme evolution; memes inversely shape gene expression

### Axiom 13: Extended Phenotype Principle
**Social systems are the "extended bodies" constructed by gene groups at the cultural level to maximize their own fitness.**
- **Biological Origin**: Proposed by Dawkins in *The Selfish Gene*
- **Social Significance**: Social systems are gene groups' collective adaptors

### Axiom 14: Negative Entropy Drive Logic Axiom
**The stability (low entropy) of social systems depends on continuously consuming individuals' economic, physiological, psychological, and informational energy.**
- **Thermodynamic Basis**: Dissipative structure theory
- **Social Significance**: System maintenance of order requires energy input

### Axiom 15: Meme Domestication Mechanism Axiom
**Systems transform discrete individual energy into group entropy reduction (order) through meme domestication.**
- **Core Mechanisms**: Selective redundancy, semantic anchoring, false subjectivity
- **Social Significance**: Deep mechanism of non-violent social control

### Axiom 16: Dynasty Cycle Law Fate Axiom
**Stability-maintenance systems follow the dynamic fate of "formation (entropy decrease) → stability (low entropy) → maturity (minimum entropy) → rigidity (entropy increase) → collapse (entropy explosion)."**
- **System Dynamics**: Entropy change patterns
- **Historical Significance**: Explaining the underlying logic of dynasty rise and fall

### Axiom 17: Infinite Evolution Conditions Axiom
**Breaking through the cycle law must simultaneously satisfy five conditions: infinite energy flow, information openness, meme renewal, power decentralization, and self-repair.**
- **Systems Science**: Complex adaptive systems theory
- **Future Significance**: Necessary conditions for sustained civilizational evolution
