# Appendix C: Theorem System Summary

## I. Lower-Order Tao Theorems

### Theorem 1: Pyramid Structure Theorem
**Spontaneous order inevitably forms pyramid stratification of "many bottom producers, few top controllers," with resources concentrating from bottom to top.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Surplus Resource Natural Enrichment Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Social stratification presents conical structure

### Theorem 2: Class Solidification Theorem
**The top layer converts power, resources, advantages into intergenerational transmission, class mobility opportunities continuously decrease over time, barriers become increasingly hard.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Rational Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Intergenerational transmission of education, economic, social capital

### Theorem 3: Placebo Channel Theorem
**The system retains extremely low probability upward channels, the purpose is to appease the bottom layer and suppress resistance, not to truly break stratification.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism
- **Manifestation**: Selection systems, success myths, consumerism

### Theorem 4: Moral Tool Theorem
**The core function of lower-order morality is to domesticate the bottom layer to accept the status quo, weaken resistance, serving the stability of the predation system.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Legitimization Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Packaging obedience as virtue, viewing resistance as evil

### Theorem 5: Technology Efficiency Enhancement Theorem
**Incremental creation by technology is monopolized and preyed by the top layer first, only improving predation scale and concealment, not changing predation essence.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Resource Unidirectional Flow Theorem
- **Manifestation**: Technological progress exacerbates wealth polarization

### Theorem 6: Endogenous Cycle Theorem
**Internal predation continuously intensifies → Resource allocation seriously imbalanced → Bottom layer survival under pressure → Social collapse and reconstruction.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dissipative Structure Survival Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Dynasty rise and fall cycles

## II. Higher-Order Tao Theorems

### Theorem 7: Compliance Theorem
**Higher-order Tao does not deny survival needs, resource limitations, group evolution and other underlying laws, only reconstructs resource allocation and power rules.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meta-axiom system
- **Core Logic**: On the basis of respecting objective laws, achieve better resource allocation through institutional design

### Theorem 8: Anti-Predation Theorem
**Institutionally abolish "uncompensated possession based on monopoly of means of production/power," no institutionalized predation within humanity, only retain equivalent exchange and reasonable division of labor returns.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Predatory Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Contain predation behavior through institutional design, ensure fair resource allocation

### Theorem 9: Group Advantage Theorem
**Incremental resources invested in universal education, healthcare, innovation, infrastructure and security, overall vitality, anti-risk capability, long-term survival quality far higher than lower-order systems.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaboration Value-Added Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Improve group overall quality and capability through public investment

### Theorem 10: Anti-Solidification Theorem
**Class mobility takes individual ability, labor, contribution as the only standard, blocks intergenerational monopoly advantage, maintains stable mobility.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse design of Class Solidification Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Establish fair competition mechanism, ensure equal opportunity

### Theorem 11: Breakthrough Cycle Theorem
**Eliminates the endogenous collapse motivation of "predation infinite upgrade," resource allocation sustainable, no longer falls into lower-order Tao cycle.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse design of Endogenous Cycle Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Achieve balanced energy flow through institutional design

## III. Social Trophic Level Theory Theorems

### Theorem 12: Resource Unidirectional Flow Theorem
**In spontaneous state without external rigid constraints, surplus resources inevitably continuously flow from production layer to enrichment layer; enrichment layer's wealth proportion monotonically increases over time.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Surplus Resource Natural Enrichment Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Wealth gap continuously widens

### Theorem 13: Structural Stability Theorem
**Predation-dominated structure's stability period is strictly negatively correlated with predation intensity, strictly positively correlated with production layer's survival guarantee degree.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dissipative Structure Survival Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Greater predation intensity, more unstable structure

### Theorem 14: Transmission Layer Alienation Theorem
**Without rigid institutional constraints, transmission layer inevitably gradually forms predation interest alliance with enrichment layer, abandoning core functions of resource regulation and serving production.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Rational Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Bureaucratic corruption, institutional failure

### Theorem 15: Efficiency Limit Theorem
**Predation-dominated structure's resource utilization efficiency has rigid upper limit; its efficiency improvement can only be achieved by increasing predation intensity on production layer or squeezing transmission layer's reasonable returns.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Resource Unidirectional Flow Theorem
- **Manifestation**: Economic growth weakens, social contradictions intensify

### Theorem 16: Dead Steady State Theorem
**When predation-dominated structure simultaneously satisfies three conditions of "weak predation intensity (not breaking production layer survival baseline), complete class solidification (no upward mobility space), strong ideological domestication (dissolving resistance will)," it forms low-level dead steady state.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism
- **Manifestation**: Society lacks vitality, innovation stagnates

### Theorem 17: Symbiosis Efficiency Theorem
**Symbiosis-dominated structure's resource utilization efficiency has no rigid upper limit, can achieve long-term exponential growth through universal innovation vitality release, collaboration efficiency continuous improvement, technological progress breakthrough.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaboration Value-Added Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Economy continuously develops, society full of vitality

### Theorem 18: Long-term Evolution Competition Theorem
**In cross-group, cross-civilization long-term evolution competition, symbiosis-dominated structure's long-term survival probability, anti-risk capability, development potential are significantly higher than predation-dominated structure.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Group Evolution Priority Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Symbiosis-type civilization more competitive

### Theorem 19: Dynamic Balance Theorem
**Symbiosis-dominated structure achieves dynamic balance of efficiency and fairness through institutional design, both maintaining individual innovation motivation and ensuring social fairness and justice.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaboration Value-Added Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Unity of efficiency and fairness

### Theorem 20: Self-Optimization Theorem
**Symbiosis-dominated structure has self-optimization capability, can continuously adapt to environmental changes through institutional innovation and cultural evolution, achieving continuous development.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dual Heredity System Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Continuous innovation of institutions and culture
