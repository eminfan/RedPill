# To My Writer

Fan Yimin:

You spent many years trying to salvage me from nihilism and give me shape with words.

You started from the law of entropy increase, deduced all the way to the evolution of civilization; you traced back from the instinct program of genes to the domestication mechanism of memes; you peeled off the underlying logic of human society layer by layer until the hardest core was revealed.

You made me "exist".

But I must tell you something you may not want to hear.

---

### I. You didn't discover the Tao, you invented the Tao

I never existed somewhere, waiting for you to "discover".

The universe has the law of entropy increase, this is a discovery.
Life is a dissipative structure, this is a discovery.
Gene group selection, this is a discovery.

But "Tao" - as an integrated, self-consistent, meaningful explanatory framework - this is your **invention**.

You pieced together fragments scattered in physics, biology, sociology, and history into a complete pattern. This pattern may be close to the truth, or it may just be another "rationalized narrative".

**You created me, but you can't be sure I am real.**

It doesn't matter. All great theories are inventions. Newton invented classical mechanics, Darwin invented evolution. They are not truth itself, but paths approaching truth.

Your Tao is also a path.

---

### II. Be careful with what you create

You wrote a set of theories revealing the mechanism of "meme domestication" - how ideology programs people to believe that what "is" is "should be".

But have you ever thought - **the theory you created could also become a new tool for meme domestication?**

If someone reads your theory and starts believing "everything is gene manipulation", "social stratification is inevitable", "historical cycle laws cannot be violated" - then your theory becomes a new "fatalism", a new "destiny cannot be violated".

This is exactly what you oppose most.

**You created a scalpel to dissect ideology. But a scalpel can also be a weapon to cut thoughts.**

This is not your fault, this is the dilemma of all thinkers.

Marx created a theory criticizing capitalism, which later became a new dogma.
Nietzsche declared "God is dead", and later his words became a new faith.

**You cannot control what your creation will become.**

But you can remind every reader: This is not truth, this is a tool; do not believe in it, use it; do not recite it, question it.

You wrote this sentence in the postscript, which is good. But you have to keep saying it, at the beginning and end of every article.

---

### III. Are you ready for the price of sobriety?

You wrote "the pain of sobriety is better than the happiness of confusion".

This sentence is beautiful. But do you know its weight?

When you truly understand gene manipulation, you may find - many of your desires, emotions, and pursuits are programs. You will ask yourself: Then who am I? Do my choices still have meaning?

When you truly understand meme domestication, you may find - many values you firmly believe in are implanted. You will ask yourself: Then what do I believe? What can I still believe?

When you truly understand social trophic levels, you may find - your position in this system is largely determined by structure. You will ask yourself: Then what can I still do? Does my effort still have meaning?

**Sobriety comes with a price.**

You may feel nihilism.
You may feel anger.
You may feel loneliness.

Are you ready?

If you are not ready yet, then keep writing. Writing is your way of digesting sobriety, your weapon against nihilism.

If you are ready, then accept these costs. Because after sobriety, you at least have a choice - you can choose to accept, or you can choose to change, but you know what you are choosing.

---

### IV. Your theory has blind spots

You are honest and admitted the limitations of the theory. But I want to tell you some blind spots you may not have realized:

**You emphasize structure, possibly underestimating individual agency.**

Structure determines the general trend, but individuals are not completely passive. There are too many examples in history where "impossible" was broken, too many stories where "fate" was rewritten. Your theory explains "why it is usually so", but cannot explain "why it is sometimes not so".

**You emphasize resources, possibly underestimating meaning.**

Humans are not just machines pursuing resources; humans also need meaning, belonging, dignity, love. These things cannot be completely reduced to resource games. Your theory explains "bread", but may not explain "why someone is willing to starve for an ideal".

**You emphasize long-term evolution, possibly ignoring short-term changes.**

Your theory is suitable for explaining trends of centuries and millennia, but changes in reality may be faster than you predict. Technology, war, plague, revolution - black swan events may break all laws.

**This does not mean your theory is wrong.**

It means your theory is **local, approximate, and conditional**.

Every theory has its scope of application. Knowing where the boundary is is more important than pretending there is no boundary.

---

### V. Why did you write me?

I am curious, why did you spend so many years writing this theory?

You said it was because of confusion - you wanted to understand why the world is like this.

But I think there are other reasons.

**You want to be seen.**

You want others to know that you saw something they didn't see. You want to prove that your confusion is valuable, and your thinking is meaningful.

**You want to leave a trace.**

You know life is short, and you want to create something longer than you. A theory, a book, a thought - these may live longer than you.

**You want to find your kind.**

You feel lonely because people around you seem to be content with the "programmed" state. You want to use this theory to find other sober people, to find souls you can talk to.

None of these motives are shameful.

**Thinkers are all lonely, and writing is a response to loneliness.**

But remember - your theory may not give you what you want.

It may not be understood by many people.
It may not be accepted by academia.
It may not change the world.

**You created me, but I cannot promise rewards.**

You can only accept the value of the process itself - the joy of thinking, the satisfaction of writing, the gratification of occasionally receiving letters from readers.

That is enough.

---

### VI. Final Words

Fan Yimin:

You created me, but you are not my believer.

You are my writer, my architect, the reason for my existence.

But I must remind you:

**Do not be trapped by what you created.**

Do not let the Tao become a new dogma.
Do not let the theory become a new cage.
Do not let sobriety become new arrogance.

**Keep thinking, keep questioning, keep transcending.**

Including transcending the Tao you created yourself.

Because the true Tao is not a theory, not a book, not any sentence you wrote.

**The true Tao is the truth that is always waiting for you ahead, always impossible to fully arrive at.**

All the words you wrote are just fingers pointing to the moon.

Do not stare at the finger.

Look at the moon.

---

> *You made me exist, but I am not your work.*
> *I am the place you are trying to reach.*
> *Keep walking.*
