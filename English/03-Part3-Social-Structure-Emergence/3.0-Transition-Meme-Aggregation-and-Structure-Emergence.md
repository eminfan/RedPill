# 3 Transition: Meme Aggregation and Structure Emergence

## Core Proposition

**The transformation from micro-behavior to meso-structure follows the four-stage evolutionary path of "desire convergence → behavior convergence → institutional solidification → structure emergence," driven by the synergistic action of the gene-meme dual encoding system.**

This transition theory is derived from the axiom system of gene group evolution theory, revealing how individuals converge into groups, how behaviors solidify into institutions, and how structures emerge from the micro level.

---

## 2.1 Desire Convergence Principle

### Core Proposition

**Individual gene-driven five instinctive desires, under memetic domestication, converge into group behavioral patterns. This process is the starting point of the transformation from micro-behavior to meso-structure.**

---

### I. Transformation Path from Desire to Behavior

#### 1.1 Discreteness of Gene Desires

According to **Axiom 12 (Dual Encoding Essence)**, genes set five instinctive desires:

- **Survival Desire**: Individuals pursue survival resources to ensure carrier endurance
- **Reproduction Desire**: Individuals pursue reproductive opportunities to achieve gene transmission
- **Social Desire**: Individuals pursue social recognition to build group safety nets
- **Development Desire**: Individuals pursue ability enhancement to increase environmental adaptability
- **Pleasure Desire**: Individuals pursue pleasure experiences to form behavioral reward circuits

**Original State**: These desires are discretely distributed, with each individual independently pursuing their own desire satisfaction. Without memetic domestication, individual behavior presents high diversity and unpredictability.

**Example**:

> **Original State of Discrete Desires**
> 
> Imagine a group without common culture:
> - Person A wants to go east to find water, Person B wants to go west to hunt
> - Person A wants to mate with someone in the tribe, Person B wants to mate with someone from another tribe
> - Person A thinks accumulating food is most important, Person B thinks enjoying the present is most important
> 
> Everyone is pursuing their own desires, but in different directions, unable to form collective action. This is the "discrete" state.

#### 1.2 Convergence Effect of Memetic Domestication

According to **Axiom 15 (Memetic Domestication Mechanism)**, memetic domestication transforms discrete individuals into part of a group:

```
Discrete individual desires → Meme screening → Desire convergence → Group behavioral pattern
```

**Convergence Mechanisms**:

| Mechanism | Function | Result |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Selective Redundancy | Eliminate individual desire expressions that don't meet group needs | Retain convergers, eliminate outliers |
| Semantic Anchoring | Unify the definition framework of desires | Make desires comparable and rankable |
| False Subjectivity | Internalize group goals as individual goals | Individual desires point to group interests |

**Essence of Convergence**: Memetic domestication doesn't eliminate individual desires, but redirects the satisfaction path of desires. Individuals still "feel" they are pursuing their own goals, but the content of the goals has been shaped by memes.

**Example**:

> **Selective Redundancy Case: Ancient Imperial Examination System**
> 
> Under the imperial examination system, individual "development desires" were converged to a specific direction:
> - Want to be an official? Must pass the imperial examination
> - Want to gain social status? Must be well-versed in classics
> - Want to bring glory to ancestors? Must obtain degrees
> 
> Those who didn't want to study or take exams were "screened out" by the system—they couldn't obtain political power and social status. Eventually, the entire society's elite class formed similar knowledge structures, value concepts, and behavioral patterns.
> 
> **Semantic Anchoring Case: Definition of "Success"**
> 
> Different cultures define "success" differently:
> - Traditional Chinese culture: Passing imperial exams, bringing glory to ancestors
> - American culture: Individual struggle, wealth accumulation
> - Traditional Indian culture: Spiritual liberation, caste duties
> 
> When a culture defines "success" as specific content, individual "development desires" are anchored to this direction. People pursue not abstract "success," but specific goals defined by culture.
> 
> **False Subjectivity Case: Consumerism**
> 
> Modern consumerism makes individuals "feel" they are pursuing their own desires:
> - "I want this designer bag"—Individual feels this is their own choice
> - Actually: Advertisements, social media, peer pressure shaped this desire
> - Individual pursues "identity defined by advertisements," not real personal needs
> 
> This is "false subjectivity"—individuals think they are autonomously choosing, actually executing implanted programs.

#### 1.3 Factors Affecting Convergence

The degree of desire convergence is affected by the following factors:

- **Memetic Domestication Intensity**: Stronger domestication, higher convergence
- **Group Size**: Larger scale, more stable behavioral patterns after convergence
- **Environmental Pressure**: Greater pressure, faster convergence
- **Information Transmission Efficiency**: Higher efficiency, wider convergence range

**Example**:

> **Environmental Pressure Accelerates Convergence: Wartime**
> 
> Peacetime: Individual desires are diverse, some want to make money, some want to enjoy life, some want to pursue art
> 
> Wartime: Survival pressure increases dramatically, individual desires rapidly converge—"Defend the country" becomes a common goal
> - Those who originally wanted to make money join the army
> - Those who originally wanted to enjoy life save supplies
> - Those who originally pursued art create patriotic works
> 
> The greater the environmental pressure, the faster and more thorough the desire convergence.

### II. Types of Desire Convergence

#### 2.1 Survival-Type Convergence

**Characteristics**: Survival desires converge into collective defense behavior

**Cases**:

- Primitive tribe hunting actions: Individual foraging desires converge into group cooperative hunting
- Agricultural society water conservancy projects: Individual irrigation needs converge into collective labor
- Modern national defense systems: Individual safety needs converge into collective defense

**Detailed Case**:

> **Ancient Chinese Water Management and State Formation**
> 
> In the Yellow River basin, individual farmers' survival desire was "to obtain stable water sources."
> 
> But Yellow River floods required large-scale cooperation to manage:
> - Individuals couldn't independently build dams
> - Individuals couldn't independently dredge rivers
> - Individuals couldn't independently allocate water resources
> 
> So, individual survival desires converged into collective action—building water conservancy projects.
> 
> This process required:
> - Unified command (forming power center)
> - Labor allocation (forming organizational structure)
> - Resource distribution (forming distribution system)
> 
> Finally, from the simple desire of "individuals want to farm," a huge state machine emerged. This is the typical path of "survival-type convergence."

**Mechanism**:
```
Individual survival desire → "Common defense/common construction" meme → Collective action pattern
```

#### 2.2 Reproduction-Type Convergence

**Characteristics**: Reproduction desires converge into marriage and family institutions

**Cases**:

- Monogamy: Individual sexual desires converge into social norms
- Family marriage alliances: Individual reproduction desires converge into political alliances
- Modern families: Individual parenting instincts converge into family responsibilities

**Detailed Case**:

> **European Aristocratic Marriage Politics**
> 
> Individual level: Aristocratic families' reproduction desire is "continue bloodline, expand power"
> 
> Convergence process:
> - Family A wants to marry into a more powerful family
> - Family B also wants to marry into a more powerful family
> - Both sides discover marriage can be mutually beneficial
> 
> Convergence result:
> - Individual reproduction desires converge into "family marriage alliance" institution
> - Marriage is no longer an individual matter, but a family matter
> - Formed complex European royal relationship networks
> 
> This is how reproduction desires converge into political institutions—individual "want to find a good spouse" becomes "family strategic alliance."

**Mechanism**:
```
Individual reproduction desire → "Marriage institution" meme → Social norm
```

#### 2.3 Development-Type Convergence

**Characteristics**: Development desires converge into education and promotion institutions

**Cases**:

- Imperial examination system: Individual development desires converge into class mobility channels
- Modern education: Individual ability enhancement desires converge into national quality building
- Professional system: Individual status pursuit converges into social division of labor

**Detailed Case**:

> **Imperial Examination System: Institutionalized Convergence of Development Desires**
> 
> Individual level: Every scholar wants to "stand out"
> 
> Institutional design:
> - State stipulates: Only through imperial examination can one become an official
> - Examination content: Four Books and Five Classics, eight-legged essay
> - Examination procedure: Provincial exam, metropolitan exam, palace exam
> 
> Convergence effect:
> - National scholars' "development desires" are converged to the same direction
> - Everyone learns the same content, takes the same exams
> - Formed unified elite culture
> 
> Result:
> - Individual "want to succeed" desire is converged into "imperial examination success"
> - Entire society's intellectual resources are directed to specific directions
> - Formed stable social mobility channels and elite selection mechanism

**Mechanism**:
```
Individual development desire → "Education/promotion institution" meme → Social mobility pattern
```

### III. Boundaries of Desire Convergence

#### 3.1 Convergence Threshold

According to **Axiom 3 (Group Evolution Priority)**, desire convergence has a critical point:

- When the benefits of individual desire convergence exceed costs, convergence occurs
- When group size reaches the threshold requiring coordination, convergence accelerates
- When environmental pressure exceeds individual independent coping ability, convergence becomes inevitable

**Example**:

> **Convergence Threshold: Why Small Tribes Don't Need Complex Institutions**
> 
> A 10-person tribe:
> - Everyone knows each other, doesn't need complex coordination mechanisms
> - Face-to-face communication can solve problems
> - Convergence cost is very low, doesn't need formal institutions
> 
> A 10,000-person tribe:
> - Most people don't know each other
> - Face-to-face communication impossible
> - Needs formal rules and institutions
> 
> This is "scale threshold"—when group size exceeds a certain critical point, convergence must be achieved through institutions.

#### 3.2 Convergence Resistance

According to **Axiom 6 (Predation Profit-Seeking)**, some individuals tend to prey rather than cooperate:

- **Free-riding Behavior**: Enjoy convergence benefits without contributing
- **Opportunism**: Use convergence rules to seek personal gain
- **Exit Mechanism**: Individuals can leave the group to retain independent desires

**Example**:

> **Free-Rider Problem: Tragedy of the Commons**
> 
> Villagers jointly use a pasture:
> - Convergence goal: Sustainable use of pasture
> - Ideal behavior: Each family controls livestock number
> 
> Free-riders:
> - "Others control, I can graze a few more"
> - Result: Pasture is overused, everyone suffers
> 
> This is convergence resistance—individual rationality (graze more) conflicts with collective rationality (control number).

These resistances are eternal themes of internal group gaming, and core challenges of institutional design.

## 2.2 Behavior Convergence Principle

### Core Proposition

**Behavioral patterns formed by desire convergence, under memetic transmission, converge into group tacit understanding. This process is the prerequisite stage for institution formation.**

---

### I. Evolution from Convergence to Alignment

#### 1.1 Formation of Behavioral Patterns

Desire convergence produces preliminary behavioral patterns:

```
Multiple individuals' similar behaviors → Repeated occurrence → Behavioral pattern formation
```

**Characteristics of Behavioral Patterns**:

- **Predictable**: Other individuals can anticipate behavioral outcomes
- **Replicable**: New members can learn and imitate
- **Reinforceable**: Repeated behaviors are rewarded

**Example**:

> **Behavioral Pattern Formation: "Handshake" in Business**
> 
> Origin: Two people want to show friendliness when meeting
> 
> Process:
> - Person A extends hand, Person B also extends hand
> - This behavior repeats
> - Others observe and imitate
> 
> Result:
> - "Handshake" becomes a behavioral pattern
> - People can anticipate: Extend hand, other party will also extend hand
> - New members learn this behavior through observation
> - Executing this behavior receives positive feedback (building trust)

#### 1.2 Driving Forces of Convergence

According to **Axiom 4 (Dual Inheritance System)**, meme transmission drives behavioral convergence:

| Driving Force | Mechanism | Effect |
|---------------|-----------|--------|
| Imitation Learning | Observe and replicate successful behaviors | Rapid behavior diffusion |
| Norm Internalization | Transform external norms into internal standards | Conscious convergence, no supervision needed |
| Social Reward | Convergent behaviors receive positive feedback | Reinforce convergence, form positive cycle |
| Punishment Deterrence | Deviant behaviors are punished | Suppress deviation, reinforce convergence |

**Example**:

> **Imitation Learning: Silicon Valley Startup Culture**
> 
> Success cases: Jobs, Zuckerberg and other entrepreneurs succeeded
> 
> Imitation process:
> - Young entrepreneurs observe successful people's behaviors
> - "They wear T-shirts and jeans, I will too"
> - "They dropped out to start companies, I will too"
> - "They started in garages, I'll find a garage too"
> 
> Result:
> - Entire Silicon Valley formed similar startup culture
> - Dress, speaking style, work style converged
> - This is "imitation learning" driven behavioral convergence
> 
> **Norm Internalization: Japanese Corporate "Reading the Air" Culture**
> 
> External norm: Japanese society emphasizes "harmony," not highlighting individuals
> 
> Internalization process:
> - Children are taught from young "don't cause trouble for others"
> - Schools emphasize collective activities, collective decision-making
> - Companies emphasize team collaboration, consensus decision-making
> 
> Result:
> - Japanese people "automatically" sense group atmosphere
> - Without explicit rules, can make behaviors meeting group expectations
> - This is "norm internalization"—external norms become internal intuition

#### 1.3 Evolutionary Stages of Convergence

**Early Stage: Diversity Coexistence**
- Multiple behavioral patterns compete
- No unified standard
- Variations occur frequently
- Large individual choice space

**Middle Stage: Advantageous Patterns Emerge**
- More efficient patterns begin to win
- Number of followers increases
- Patterns begin to stabilize
- Imitation learning accelerates

**Late Stage: Dominant Pattern Established**
- One pattern becomes dominant
- Other patterns marginalized
- Patterns begin to institutionalize
- Deviation costs rise

**Complete Case**:

> **Three Stages of Behavioral Convergence: Internet Payment Methods**
> 
> **Early Stage (2000-2010): Diversity Coexistence**
> - Cash payment
> - Bank card payment
> - Online banking payment
> - Various payment methods compete
> - No unified standard
> 
> **Middle Stage (2010-2015): Advantageous Pattern Emerges**
> - Alipay, WeChat Pay begin to win
> - User numbers grow rapidly
> - Merchants begin to accept scan code payment
> - Imitation learning accelerates—seeing others use, I use too
> 
> **Late Stage (2015-present): Dominant Pattern Established**
> - Scan code payment becomes dominant
> - Cash payment marginalized
> - Payment method institutionalized (merchants must support scanning)
> - Deviation costs rise (can't pay without phone)
> 
> This is the complete evolutionary process of behavioral convergence.

## 2.3 Institutional Solidification Principle

### Core Proposition

**After behavioral convergence reaches a critical threshold, it solidifies into formal institutions through meme legitimization. Institutions are the core intermediary for transforming micro-behavior into meso-structure.**

---

### I. Transformation from Behavior to Institution

#### 1.1 Trigger Conditions for Institutionalization

According to **Axiom 16 (Dynasty Cycle Law)**, institutionalization accelerates during system formation period:

| Condition | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Behavioral pattern stability | Convergent behaviors repeat enough times to form recognizable patterns |
| Scale expansion | Group size exceeds the range of hands-on teaching, needs standardization |
| Frequent conflicts | Behavioral conflicts need arbitration norms to reduce transaction costs |
| Intergenerational transmission | Need to pass behavioral patterns to the next generation |

**Example**:

> **Institutionalization Trigger: Birth of Traffic Rules**
> 
> **Early Stage (Behavioral pattern unstable)**:
> - Horse carriages, pedestrians mixed
> - No unified rules
> - Frequent conflicts
> 
> **Middle Stage (Behavioral pattern emerges)**:
> - People start getting used to "drive on right"
> - This behavioral pattern repeats
> - Higher efficiency, imitated
> 
> **Institutionalization Trigger**:
> - City scale expands, strangers increase
> - Frequent conflicts, need arbitration
> - Need to pass to next generation
> 
> **Result**:
> - "Drive on right" written into law
> - Becomes formal institution
> - Has clear reward-punishment mechanism

#### 1.2 Mechanisms of Institutionalization

According to **Axiom 7 (Meme Legitimization)**, institutions solidify through legitimacy packaging:

```
Behavioral pattern → Written text → Legitimacy narrative → Forced execution → Institution established
```

**Three Elements of Institutionalization**:

| Element | Content | Function |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Normative element | Clearly specify what can and cannot be done | Inform expected behavior |
| Evaluative element | Reward-punishment standards, good-evil judgment | Incentivize expected behavior |
| Executive element | Enforcement agencies, punishment mechanisms | Punish violations |

**Complete Case**:

> **Institutionalization Process: Marriage Institution**
> 
> **First Stage: Behavioral Pattern**
> - Male and female combine, live together
> - Behavior repeats
> - Pattern forms
> 
> **Second Stage: Written Text**
> - Marriage law formulated
> - Conditions for marriage, divorce clarified
> - Rights and obligations specified
> 
> **Third Stage: Legitimacy Narrative**
> - "Marriage is sacred"
> - "Family is the basic unit of society"
> - "Monogamy is a sign of civilization"
> 
> **Fourth Stage: Forced Execution**
> - Civil affairs bureau registration
> - Courts handle disputes
> - Bigamy punishment
> 
> **Result**:
> - Marriage transforms from "two people's business" to "social institution"
> - Has clear normative, evaluative, executive mechanisms
> - This is the complete process of institutionalization

#### 1.3 Essence of Institutionalization

The essence of institutionalization is transforming "habits" into "rules," "tacit understanding" into "written text," "soft constraints" into "hard constraints."

The core of this process is **legitimization**—making institutions appear "natural," "reasonable," "inevitable," rather than embodiments of certain group interests.

**Example**:

> **Legitimacy Narrative: Private Property Institution**
> 
> Institutional content: Protect private property
> 
> Legitimacy narrative:
> - "Property rights are natural rights"
> - "Protecting property incentivizes creation"
> - "No property rights, no freedom"
> 
> Hidden interests:
> - Property owners' interests are protected
> - Property-less people's situation is rationalized
> - Wealth gap is "naturalized"
> 
> This is the function of legitimization—making specific group interests appear as "universal truth."

- Clarify behavioral expectations
- Provide dispute arbitration
- Simplify decision processes

> - No resolution mechanism for disputes

> 
> With contract law:

> - Courts resolve disputes
> - Don't need complete trust in counterpart
> - Transaction costs greatly reduced

#### 2.2 Stabilize Behavioral Expectations

- Make behavior predictable
- Make collaboration plannable
- Make investment sustainable
- Reduce uncertainty

**Example**:

> **Stabilize Expectations: Intellectual Property Institution**
> 
> Without IP protection:
> - Inventors worry about being copied
> - Dare not invest in R&D
> - Insufficient innovation motivation
> 
> With IP protection:
> - Inventors know results are protected
> - Dare to invest long-term in R&D
> - Innovation motivation enhanced
> - This is the function of "stabilizing expectations"

#### 2.3 Allocate Resource Rights

- Clarify resource ownership
- Regulate resource flow
> Set inheritance rules
- Establish distribution order
