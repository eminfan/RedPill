## 13.1 Rise and Fall Cycles of Chinese Dynasties

### Case Background

The rise and fall cycles of ancient Chinese dynasties are typical cases of the historical cycle law. From the Qin Dynasty to the Qing Dynasty, China experienced multiple dynasty replacements, with each dynasty's life cycle approximately 200-300 years.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Operating Mechanism of Predatory Tiandao

1. **Formation of Pyramid Structure**:
   - After a new dynasty is established, a pyramid structure forms with the emperor at the apex
   - The bottom layer consists of the broad peasantry, the middle layer is the bureaucratic class, and the top layer is the imperial family and nobility

2. **Process of Class Solidification**:
   - Early period: Relatively fair land distribution, strong social mobility
   - Middle period: Land consolidation begins, classes gradually solidify
   - Late period: Land highly concentrated, class mobility nearly stagnant

3. **Operation of Placebo Channels**:
   - Imperial examination system: Providing upward channels for the bottom layer
   - Confucian ideology: Emphasizing hierarchical order, domesticating the bottom layer

4. **Energy Imbalance and Collapse**:
   - Land consolidation causes peasants to lose their land
   - Tax burden increases, bottom-layer living conditions deteriorate
   - Peasant uprisings erupt, dynasty collapses

### Specific Case: Han Dynasty

- **Formation Period** (202 BCE - 141 BCE):
  - Liu Bang establishes the Han Dynasty, implementing recuperation policies
  - Relatively fair land distribution, social economy recovers

- **Stability Period** (140 BCE - 49 BCE):
  - Rule of Wen and Jing, economic prosperity
  - Emperor Wu's reign, national power reaches its peak
  - Salt and iron monopolies begin, strengthening centralization

- **Maturity Period** (48 BCE - 8 CE):
  - Zhaoxuan Revival, social stability
  - Land consolidation begins to intensify

- **Rigidity Period** (9 CE - 23 CE):
  - Wang Mang usurps the Han, implements reforms
  - Social contradictions intensify, Chimei and Green Forest uprisings

- **Collapse Period** (23 CE - 25 CE):
  - Wang Mang's regime is overthrown
  - Liu Xiu establishes the Eastern Han

### Theoretical Verification

- **Endogenous Cycle Theorem**: The rise and fall of the Han Dynasty verifies the cycle law of predatory Tiandao
- **Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem**: Land concentrating from peasants to landlords verifies the pattern of unidirectional resource flow
- **Transmission Layer Alienation Theorem**: The bureaucratic class and landlord class form an interest alliance, jointly preying on peasants

## 13.2 Rise and Fall of the Roman Empire

### Case Background

The Roman Empire, from its establishment in 27 BCE to the fall of the Western Roman Empire in 476 CE, experienced approximately 500 years of history, similarly following the historical cycle law.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Operating Mechanism of Predatory Tiandao

1. **Formation of Pyramid Structure**:
   - After the Roman Empire was established, a pyramid structure formed with the emperor at the apex
   - The bottom layer consisted of slaves and free citizens, the middle layer of knights and bureaucrats, and the top layer of the imperial family and nobility

2. **Process of Class Solidification**:
   - Early period: Citizenship expanded, strong social mobility
   - Middle period: Land concentrated, slavery system developed
   - Late period: Classes solidified, social contradictions intensified

3. **Operation of Placebo Channels**:
   - Citizenship system: Providing upward channels for free citizens
   - Roman law: Emphasizing equality before the law, domesticating the bottom layer

4. **Energy Imbalance and Collapse**:
   - Slavery system led to low production efficiency
   - External expansion stopped, losing new resource sources
   - Barbarian invasions, empire collapsed

### Specific Case: Roman Empire

- **Formation Period** (27 BCE - 96 CE):
  - Augustus establishes the Roman Empire
  - Implements the Principate, strengthens centralization
  - External expansion, obtains massive resources

- **Stability Period** (96 CE - 192 CE):
  - Five Good Emperors period, political stability, economic prosperity
  - Cultural development, Rome reaches its zenith

- **Maturity Period** (193 CE - 235 CE):
  - Severan Dynasty, military expansion
  - Social contradictions begin to accumulate

- **Rigidity Period** (235 CE - 284 CE):
  - Third Century Crisis, political turmoil
  - Economic decline, severe inflation

- **Collapse Period** (284 CE - 476 CE):
  - Diocletian and Constantine reforms
  - Fall of the Western Roman Empire

### Theoretical Verification

- **Endogenous Cycle Theorem**: The rise and fall of the Roman Empire verifies the cycle law of predatory Tiandao
- **Technology Efficiency Enhancement Theorem**: Technological progress did not change the essence of predation, ultimately leading to system collapse
- **External Shock**: Barbarian invasions accelerated the Roman Empire's collapse

## 13.3 Trophic Level Analysis of the Indian Caste System

### Case Background

The Indian caste system is one of the oldest and most enduring social stratification systems in the world, originating in the Vedic period and still profoundly influencing Indian society today.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Shudras: Engaged in physical labor, such as farmers, artisans, servants
   - Untouchables (Dalits): Engaged in the lowest labor, such as cleaning, handling corpses

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Vaishyas: Engaged in commerce, handicrafts, and agriculture
   - Some Kshatriyas: Lower-ranking warriors and officials

3. **Third Trophic Level · Enrichment Layer**:
   - Brahmins: Priests, scholars, teachers
   - Kshatriyas: Kings, warriors, nobles

#### Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - Shudras and Dalits create surplus resources
   - Resources flow upward through Vaishyas and lower Kshatriyas
   - Ultimately enriching Brahmins and higher Kshatriyas

2. **Meme Domestication**:
   - Hindu teachings: Sanctifying the caste system
   - Reincarnation concept: Current status is the result of past-life karma
   - Untouchability system: Reinforcing class boundaries

3. **Dead Steady State**:
   - The caste system forms a low-level dead steady state
   - Society lacks vitality, innovation stagnates
   - Externally closed, resisting external influence

### Theoretical Verification

- **Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem**: Surplus resources flow from Shudras and Dalits to Brahmins and Kshatriyas
- **Transmission Layer Alienation Theorem**: Vaishyas and lower Kshatriyas become tools for resource transmission
- **Dead Steady State Theorem**: The caste system forms a typical dead steady state
