## 13.1 Rise and Fall Cycles of Chinese Dynasties

### Case Background

The rise and fall cycles of ancient Chinese dynasties are typical cases of the historical cycle law. From the Qin Dynasty to the Qing Dynasty, China experienced multiple dynasty changes, each dynasty's lifecycle approximately between 200-300 years.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Operating Mechanism of Lower-Order Tao

1. **Formation of Pyramid Structure**:
   - After new dynasty establishment, pyramid structure formed with emperor at the top
   - Bottom layer is vast farmers, middle is bureaucratic class, top is royal family and nobility

2. **Process of Class Solidification**:
   - Early period: Land distribution relatively fair, social mobility relatively strong
   - Middle period: Land annexation begins, classes gradually solidify
   - Late period: Land highly concentrated, class mobility almost stagnant

3. **Operation of Placebo Channels**:
   - Imperial examination system: Provides upward channels for bottom layer
   - Confucian thought: Emphasizes hierarchical order, domesticates bottom layer

4. **Energy Imbalance and Collapse**:
   - Land annexation causes farmers to lose land
   - Tax burden increases, bottom layer life deteriorates
   - Peasant uprisings erupt, dynasty collapses

### Specific Case: Han Dynasty

- **Formation Period** (202 BC - 141 BC):
  - Liu Bang established Han Dynasty, implemented rest and recuperation policy
  - Land distribution relatively fair, social economy recovered

- **Stability Period** (140 BC - 49 BC):
  - Rule of Wen and Jing, economic prosperity
  - Emperor Wu period, national power reached peak
  - Began implementing salt and iron monopoly, strengthened centralization

- **Maturity Period** (48 BC - 8 AD):
  - Zhaoxuan Restoration, social stability
  - Land annexation began to intensify

- **Rigidity Period** (9 AD - 23 AD):
  - Wang Mang usurped Han, implemented reforms
  - Social contradictions intensified, Chimei and Green Forest uprisings

- **Collapse Period** (23 AD - 25 AD):
  - Wang Mang regime overthrown
  - Liu Xiu established Eastern Han

### Theoretical Verification

- **Endogenous Cycle Theorem**: Han Dynasty's rise and fall process verifies lower-order Tao's cycle law
- **Resource Unidirectional Flow Theorem**: Land concentrated from farmers to landlords, verifies resource unidirectional flow pattern
- **Transmission Layer Alienation Theorem**: Bureaucratic class and landlord class formed interest alliance, jointly preyed on farmers

## 13.2 Rise and Fall of Roman Empire

### Case Background

The Roman Empire from its establishment in 27 BC to the fall of Western Roman Empire in 476 AD, experienced about 500 years of history, also following the historical cycle law.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Operating Mechanism of Lower-Order Tao

1. **Formation of Pyramid Structure**:
   - After Roman Empire establishment, pyramid structure formed with emperor at the top
   - Bottom layer is slaves and free citizens, middle is knights and bureaucrats, top is royal family and nobility

2. **Process of Class Solidification**:
   - Early period: Citizenship expanded, social mobility relatively strong
   - Middle period: Land concentrated, slavery developed
   - Late period: Classes solidified, social contradictions intensified

3. **Operation of Placebo Channels**:
   - Citizenship system: Provides upward channels for free citizens
   - Roman law: Emphasizes equality before law, domesticates bottom layer

4. **Energy Imbalance and Collapse**:
   - Slavery led to low production efficiency
   - External expansion stopped, lost new resource sources
   - Barbarian invasion, empire collapsed

### Specific Case: Roman Empire

- **Formation Period** (27 BC - 96 AD):
  - Augustus established Roman Empire
  - Implemented principate, strengthened centralization
  - External expansion, obtained large resources

- **Stability Period** (96 AD - 192 AD):
  - Five Good Emperors period, political stability, economic prosperity
  - Cultural development, Rome reached peak

- **Maturity Period** (193 AD - 235 AD):
  - Severan dynasty, military expansion
  - Social contradictions began to accumulate

- **Rigidity Period** (235 AD - 284 AD):
  - Third Century Crisis, political turmoil
  - Economic decline, serious inflation

- **Collapse Period** (284 AD - 476 AD):
  - Diocletian and Constantine reforms
  - Western Roman Empire fell

### Theoretical Verification

- **Endogenous Cycle Theorem**: Roman Empire's rise and fall process verifies lower-order Tao's cycle law
- **Technology Efficiency Enhancement Theorem**: Technological progress didn't change predation essence, eventually led to system collapse
- **External Shock**: Barbarian invasion accelerated Roman Empire's collapse

## 13.3 Trophic Level Analysis of Indian Caste System

### Case Background

The Indian caste system is one of the oldest and most enduring social stratification systems in the world, starting from the Vedic period, still having profound impact on Indian society today.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Shudras: Engaged in physical labor, such as farmers, craftsmen, servants
   - Untouchables (Dalits): Engaged in lowest labor, such as cleaning, handling corpses

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Vaishyas: Engaged in commerce, handicraft and agriculture
   - Some Kshatriyas: Low-level warriors and officials

3. **Third Trophic Level · Enrichment Layer**:
   - Brahmins: Priests, scholars, teachers
   - Kshatriyas: Kings, warriors, nobility

#### Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - Shudras and Dalits created surplus resources
   - Resources flowed upward through Vaishyas and low-level Kshatriyas
   - Finally enriched to Brahmins and high-level Kshatriyas

2. **Memetic Domestication**:
   - Hindu doctrine: Sacralized caste system
   - Reincarnation concept: Current life status is result of past life karma
   - Untouchability system: Reinforced class boundaries

3. **Dead Steady State**:
   - Caste system formed low-level dead steady state
   - Society lacked vitality, innovation stagnated
   - Externally closed, resisted external influence

### Theoretical Verification

- **Resource Unidirectional Flow Theorem**: Surplus resources flowed from Shudras and Dalits to Brahmins and Kshatriyas
- **Transmission Layer Alienation Theorem**: Vaishyas and low-level Kshatriyas became tools for resource transmission
- **Dead Steady State Theorem**: Caste system formed typical dead steady state
