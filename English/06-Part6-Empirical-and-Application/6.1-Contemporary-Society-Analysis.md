## 14.1 Contemporary Capitalism Analysis

### Case Background

Contemporary capitalism is the modern form of lower-order Tao, from the Industrial Revolution, experienced free capitalism, monopoly capitalism, state monopoly capitalism and other stages, currently in the financial capitalism stage.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Industrial workers, service industry practitioners, farmers
   - Bottom white-collar workers, temporary workers, gig workers

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Middle-level managers, technical personnel, professionals
   - Small and medium enterprise owners, freelancers

3. **Third Trophic Level · Enrichment Layer**:
   - Large enterprise owners, financial oligarchs, top capitalists
   - Political elites, media giants

#### Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - Production layer creates surplus value
   - Surplus value flows upward through wages, profits, interest and other forms
   - Finally enriches to financial capital and large enterprises

2. **Memetic Domestication**:
   - Neoliberal ideology: Emphasizes market freedom, individualism
   - Consumerism: Realize self-value through consumption
   - Success doctrine: Emphasizes individual struggle, ignores structural factors

3. **Placebo Channels**:
   - Education system: Provides upward mobility opportunities
   - Entrepreneurship myths: Promotes rags-to-riches success cases
   - Social welfare: Provides basic living security, alleviates social contradictions

### Contemporary Characteristics

1. **Financialization**:
   - Financial capital dominates economy, real economy relatively declines
   - Financial speculation becomes important profit source
   - Debt economy model, exacerbates economic instability

2. **Globalization**:
   - Global flow of capital, technology, labor
   - Global expansion of multinational corporations
   - Formation of global value chains

3. **Technological Revolution**:
   - Development of information technology, artificial intelligence
   - Impact of automation on employment
   - Widening digital divide

4. **Social Differentiation**:
   - Wealth gap continues to widen
   - Middle class shrinks
   - Social mobility declines

### Theoretical Verification

- **Resource Unidirectional Flow Theorem**: Surplus value flows from production layer to enrichment layer, verifies resource unidirectional flow pattern
- **Technology Efficiency Enhancement Theorem**: Increment created by technological progress is monopolized by top layer first, exacerbates wealth polarization
- **Endogenous Cycle Theorem**: Cyclical outbreaks of capitalist economic crises verify lower-order Tao's cycle law

## 14.2 Exploration of Socialism with Chinese Characteristics

### Case Background

Socialism with Chinese characteristics is exploration of higher-order Tao, from reform and opening up, experienced transformation from planned economy to market economy, currently in the new era of socialism with Chinese characteristics stage.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Industrial workers, farmers, service industry practitioners
   - Bottom layer laborers

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Middle-level managers, technical personnel, professionals
   - Small and medium enterprise owners, individual businesses

3. **Third Trophic Level · Enrichment Layer**:
   - Large enterprise owners, entrepreneurs
   - Political elites, technology elites

#### Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - Production layer creates surplus value
   - Surplus value flows through wages, profits, taxes and other forms
   - Part of surplus value used for education, healthcare, infrastructure and other public undertakings through public finance

2. **Memetic Domestication**:
   - Socialist core values: Emphasizes collectivism, fairness and justice
   - Chinese Dream: Realize great rejuvenation of Chinese nation
   - Common prosperity: Eliminate poverty, achieve common prosperity for all people

3. **Positive Circulation**:
   - Public investment: Invested in education, healthcare, infrastructure and other fields
   - Social security: Established social security system covering all people
   - Poverty alleviation: Eliminated absolute poverty

### Exploration Achievements

1. **Economic Development**:
   - Economy continues to grow rapidly, became world's second largest economy
   - Technological innovation capability continuously improves
   - Infrastructure construction achieved huge achievements

2. **Social Progress**:
   - Poverty alleviation achieved historic achievements, eliminated absolute poverty
   - Social security system continuously improves
   - Education, healthcare levels significantly improved

3. **Institutional Innovation**:
   - Public ownership as main body, multiple ownership economies developing together
   - Distribution according to work as main body, multiple distribution methods coexisting
   - Socialist market economy system

### Theoretical Verification

- **Anti-Predation Theorem**: Through institutional design, contain institutionalized predation, achieve common prosperity
- **Group Advantage Theorem**: Through public investment, improve group overall quality and capability
- **Anti-Solidification Theorem**: Through education fairness, social security and other measures, promote social mobility

## 14.3 Social Trophic Level Evolution in Technology Era

### Case Background

The technology era represented by information technology, artificial intelligence, biotechnology is profoundly changing social structure and resource allocation methods.

### Theoretical Analysis

#### Changes in Social Trophic Level Structure

1. **First Trophic Level · Production Layer**:
   - Traditional industrial workers decrease
   - Digital laborers, platform workers increase
   - Gig economy practitioners increase

2. **Second Trophic Level · Transmission Layer**:
   - Technology white-collar workers, data analysts increase
   - Traditional middle-level managers decrease
   - Freelancers, creators increase

3. **Third Trophic Level · Enrichment Layer**:
   - Technology giants, platform owners
   - Data capitalists, algorithm designers
   - Top technology experts

#### Changes in Operating Mechanism

1. **Resource Flow**:
   - Data becomes important production factor
   - Platform economy's commission model
   - Algorithm's impact on resource allocation

2. **Memetic Domestication**:
   - Algorithm recommendation: Shapes users' cognition and behavior
   - Platform rules: Control users' behavior boundaries
   - Digital identity: Constructs users' digital image

3. **Technology Empowerment and Digital Divide**:
   - Technology empowerment: Improves production efficiency, creates new employment opportunities
   - Digital divide: Differences in technology use capability lead to new social differentiation

### Challenges and Opportunities

1. **Challenges**:
   - Employment structure changes, traditional jobs decrease
   - Data monopoly, platform power too large
   - Privacy protection, personal data excessively collected
   - Algorithm bias, exacerbates social inequality

2. **Opportunities**:
   - Technological innovation, improves production efficiency
   - Remote work, increases work flexibility
   - Digital economy, creates new economic growth points
   - Education innovation, improves human capital

### Theoretical Verification

- **Technology Efficiency Enhancement Theorem**: Increment created by technological progress is monopolized by technology giants first
- **Resource Unidirectional Flow Theorem**: Data and wealth concentrate to platform owners
- **Symbiosis Possibility**: Technological progress provides new possibilities for realizing higher-order Tao

## 14.4 Common Challenges in Contemporary Society

### 1. Environmental Crisis

- Climate change: Global temperature rise, extreme weather events increase
- Resource depletion: Fossil energy, water resources and other resources shortage
- Ecological destruction: Biodiversity decreases, ecosystem degrades

### 2. Social Inequality

- Wealth gap: Global wealth gap continues to widen
- Opportunity inequality: Education, employment and other opportunity distribution uneven
- Intergenerational inequality: Resource allocation gap between generations

### 3. Technology Risks

- AI ethics: Fairness, transparency of AI decision-making
- Cybersecurity: Data breaches, cyber attacks
- Technological unemployment: Impact of automation on employment

### 4. Global Governance Dilemma

- International cooperation: Global problems require international cooperation
- Hegemonism: Unilateral behavior of individual countries
- Global justice: Fairness of global resource allocation

## 14.5 Theoretical Significance

### Diagnosis of Contemporary Society

- **System Diagnosis**: Through social trophic level analysis, diagnose contemporary society's health
- **Risk Warning**: Identify risk factors for system collapse
- **Development Direction**: Point out social development direction

### Guidance for Policy Making

- **Institutional Design**: Design systems conducive to symbiosis development
- **Policy Focus**: Focus on education fairness, social security, technological innovation
- **Global Governance**: Promote building human destiny community

### Implications for the Future

- **Technology and Institutions**: Technological progress needs institutional innovation cooperation
- **Fairness and Efficiency**: Achieve dynamic balance of fairness and efficiency
- **Global Cooperation**: Responding to global challenges requires global cooperation

## 14.6 Conclusion

Contemporary society is in the critical period of evolving from lower-order Tao to higher-order Tao. Through theoretical analysis, we can better understand contemporary society's operating mechanism, identify challenges and opportunities faced, provide reference for building fairer, more efficient, more sustainable social structures.

Technological progress provides new possibilities for human civilization upgrade, but also brings new challenges. Only through institutional innovation, cultural transformation and global cooperation can humans fully utilize technological progress dividends, achieve evolutionary leap from lower-order Tao to higher-order Tao.

Contemporary society's development proves that higher-order Tao is not an unattainable ideal, but a realistic choice based on objective laws. By recognizing and transcending lower-order Tao's limitations, humans can build a more harmonious, fair, sustainable social form, achieve continuous civilization development.
