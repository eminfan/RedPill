# 4 Three Laws of Meme Evolution

Memes follow evolutionary logic similar to genes, with three inviolable underlying laws. These three laws explain how memes propagate, evolve, and solidify in human society.

## Law One: Transmission Priority Over Truth

### Core Proposition

Meme survival does not depend on whether it conforms to facts, only on its transmission capability.

### Operational Mechanism

Memes that can trigger strong emotions, are simple and easy to understand, and conform to group psychology will always propagate faster and survive longer than rigorous, complex, neutral facts.

**Core Principle**: The human brain defaults to using "fast thinking" mode (heuristic processing); only through deliberate training can one use "slow thinking" mode (analytic processing). Memes exploit this cognitive characteristic by directly triggering emotional responses, bypassing rational analysis.

### Typical Cases

| Low-Transmission Content | High-Transmission Content |
|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| Rigorous factual analysis | Emotionally intense conspiracy theories |
| Complex logical reasoning | Simple and powerful emotional slogans |
| Objective neutral data | Moral judgment and position expression |

### Inferences

- **Fact ≠ Truth**: Memes that conform to facts do not necessarily propagate
- **Emotion is the fuel of transmission**: Strong emotions such as anger, fear, and guilt can greatly improve transmission efficiency
- **Simplicity overwhelms complexity**: Simple, crude narratives always propagate more widely than precise, complex arguments
- **Group psychology is a breeding ground**: Content that conforms to biases and group identity propagates more easily

## Law Two: Memes Cluster to Form "Meme Complexes"

### Core Proposition

A single meme has very weak control power; multiple complementary memes combine to form an extremely strong control closed loop.

### Typical Structure of a Meme Complex

```
Identity + Moral Justice + Enemy Image + Sacred Mission
     ↓           ↓          ↓          ↓
  "I am XX"   "XX is just"  "Enemies are sabotaging"  "Fight for XX"
```

### Core Characteristics of Meme Complexes

1. **Complementary Enhancement**: Each meme reinforces the control power of other memes
2. **Closed-Loop Locking**: Complete structure that cannot be broken from within
3. **Self-Reinforcement**: Hosts actively maintain and propagate the complex
4. **Exclusivity**: Strong rejection and aggression toward dissenters

### Typical Cases

| Meme Complex | Components |
|-------------|-----------|
| Extreme Religion | Identity (believer) + Justice (sacred doctrine) + Enemies (heretics) + Mission (holy war) |
| Extreme Nationalism | Identity (nation) + Justice (patriotism) + Enemies (foreign forces) + Mission (rise) |
| Fan Circle Culture | Identity (fan) + Justice (my idol is best) + Enemies (antis) + Mission (protect) |
| Ideology | Identity (class) + Justice (historical inevitability) + Enemies (reactionaries) + Mission (liberation) |

### Inferences

- **Complex > Single Meme**: The control power of a complex far exceeds the sum of individual memes
- **Deconstruction requires internal approach**: Attacking the complex from outside反而 strengthens internal cohesion
- **Beware perfect narratives**: When a narrative is "flawless," it is often a signal of a meme complex

## Law Three: Memes Evolve "Self-Protection Mechanisms"

### Core Proposition

Mature domestication memes all come with built-in defense systems, making hosts afraid to question and unable to leave.

#### Types of Self-Protection Mechanisms

##### 1. Moral Binding

Defining "questioning memes" as "immoral" behavior:
- Religion: Disbelief or questioning God is the greatest sin
- Ideology: Questioning the ideology makes you a traitor, counter-revolutionary
- Collectivism: Doubting the collective is selfish
- Fan circles: Leaving and criticizing is shameful

**Effect**: Questioners are directly morally judged, unable to gain group acceptance

##### 2. Pain Binding

Forcibly associating "leaving memes" with "painful experiences":
- Loss of faith = loss of life's meaning
- Betraying the collective = abandonment by all
- Leaving fandom = emptiness and loneliness
- Questioning authority = existential crisis

**Effect**: Leaving memes triggers strong negative emotions, forming psychological withdrawal reactions

##### 3. Enemy Narrative

Defining "counter-evidence information" as "enemy conspiracy":
- Official debunking = officials were bought off
- Objective data = data was fabricated
- Rational analysis = being brainwashed
- Different opinions = sent by enemies

**Effect**: Cutting off external information sources, forming an information cocoon

##### 4. Backfire Effect

Even if hosts are successfully exposed to counter-evidence, this反而 strengthens their belief in the original meme.

**Psychological mechanism**: When core beliefs are threatened, people resist threats by strengthening beliefs

**Effect**: Rationally attacking a meme complex only makes it more solid

### Synergistic Operation of the Three Laws

```
Transmission Priority (Diffusion)
        ↓
    Meme Complex Formation (Solidification)
        ↓
    Self-Protection Mechanism (Defense)
        ↓
    Immunity to Rational Attack (Survival)
```

These three laws together constitute the core logic of meme evolution, explaining why domestication memes are so difficult to break.

## Practical Application

### Identifying Meme Complexes

1. Observe whether there exists a "perfect narrative"—unfalsifiable, impeccable
2. Observe whether there is strong exclusivity—no questioning, no criticism allowed
3. Observe whether there is an "enemy image"—needing to constantly fight imaginary enemies
4. Observe whether there is a "sacred mission"—a sacred goal transcending personal interests

### Resisting Meme Domestication

1. Identify meme transmission mechanisms, don't be emotionally manipulated
2. Beware meme complex formation, avoid being locked into a single narrative
3. Maintain independent thinking, don't be kidnapped by self-protection mechanisms
