# 3 Human Nature Essence: Gene-Meme Dual Coding

## 3.1 Dual Coding Essence: Gene-Culture Complex

### Core Proposition

**Humans are a life continuation system jointly constituted by genes and cultural memes, co-evolving.**

This proposition subverts the traditional view of human nature, viewing humans as a complex system of dual coding:

### Dual Coding Structure

#### Gene Layer: Underlying Biological Instincts

- **Function**: Sets underlying biological instincts (five desires), provides the system's energy supply and collaboration prototype
- **Evolution Speed**: Extremely slow, in units of ten thousand years
- **Core Mechanism**: Optimizes gene frequency through natural selection
- **Manifestations**: Physiological instincts, emotional reactions, basic behavior patterns

#### Meme Layer: Upper Social Rules

- **Function**: Constructs super-biological adaptation systems (rules, institutions, culture), expands survival strategy space
- **Evolution Speed**: Extremely fast, in units of centuries, decades, or even years
- **Core Mechanism**: Optimizes meme transmission through cultural selection
- **Manifestations**: Language, morality, law, institutions, technology

### Collaboration and Conflict: Battlefield Model

The relationship between genes and memes is not just "collaboration," but "game." The human body is the **host and battlefield** of these two replicators:

1.  **Collaboration (Symbiosis)**: Memes help genes survive better (e.g., agricultural technology memes caused human population to surge).
2.  **Conflict (Parasitism)**: Memes may sacrifice gene interests for their own propagation (e.g., celibacy, martyrdom, excessive overtime).
    *   *Note: When a meme can make people sacrifice reproduction opportunities to propagate it, this meme has defeated genes.*

## 3.2 Extended Phenotype Principle: Biological Basis of Social Systems

### Core Proposition

**Social systems are "extended bodies" constructed by gene groups at the cultural layer to maximize their own fitness.**

This principle reveals the essence of social systems:

### Concept of Extended Phenotype

- **Biological Origin**: Proposed by Dawkins in "The Selfish Gene," referring to gene phenotypic expression extending beyond individual body boundaries
- **Classic Examples**: Spider webs, beaver dams, bird nests
- **Human Extension**: Human social systems are the extended phenotype of genes, the "collective body" of gene groups

### Extended Phenotype Characteristics of Social Systems

1. **Functional Service**: Social systems serve the survival and reproduction of gene groups
2. **Genetic Transmission**: Social systems are transmitted through memes, realizing the continued existence of gene groups
3. **Adaptive Evolution**: Social systems evolve with environmental changes, improving gene group fitness

### Theoretical Significance

- **Breaking Binary Opposition**: Eliminates the "biology-culture" binary opposition, viewing social systems as a natural extension of biological evolution
- **Unified Explanatory Framework**: Explains the origin and function of social phenomena from the gene level
- **Predictive Ability**: Predicts the evolutionary direction of social systems based on gene group survival needs

## 3.3 Negative Entropy Driven Logic: System's Energy Demand

### Core Proposition

**The more ordered the system, the greater the negative entropy flow required. The edifice of human civilization is built on the continuous extraction of individual biological energy.**

### Thermodynamic Explanation: The Cost of Order

According to dissipative structure theory:
$$ \Delta S_{total} = \Delta S_{system} + \Delta S_{environment} > 0 $$

- To make the **social system (System)** maintain low entropy (ordered, stable).
- Must discharge more entropy to the **environment (Environment)**.
- In sociology, **individuals often become the "environment" that discharges entropy**.

**Manifestations**:
- **996 Work System**: The system gained ordered profits (low entropy), individuals gained chaotic bodies and spirits (high entropy/overwork).
- **War**: The country gained security boundaries (order), soldiers gained death and disability (maximum entropy).

## 3.4 Three Major Derivative Mechanisms: Dynamic Game of Dual Coding

The interaction between genes and memes in the human body has derived three basic social behavior patterns:

### Mechanism 1: Meme Suppression (Superego vs Id)

- **Definition**: Memes forcibly suppress gene instincts to maintain group order.
- **Cases**:
    - **Chastity concept** (meme) suppresses **sexual desire** (gene).
    - **Integrity education** (meme) suppresses **greed** (gene).
- **Cost**: Individuals produce psychological conflicts, neuroses, repression (Freud's "price of civilization").

### Mechanism 2: Gene Rebellion (Instinct Breakthrough)

- **Definition**: Gene instincts break through meme constraints, causing social rules to fail.
- **Cases**:
    - **Exchanging children to eat** during famine (survival instinct pierces moral memes).
    - **Extramarital affairs** under strict monogamy (reproduction strategy pierces legal memes).
- **Result**: Social disorder, system entropy increase.

### Mechanism 3: False Collusion (Sacralization)

- **Definition**: Memes use gene's reward mechanism (dopamine) to induce individuals to sacrifice for the group.
- **Mechanism**:
    - Genes set the "seeking recognition" reward circuit.
    - Memes define "sacrifice is glorious."
    - Result: Individuals gain huge dopamine reward (sublimity) when sacrificing, genes think they won (gained status), memes also won (gained propagation).
- **Cases**: **Religious martyrdom**, **suicide attacks**, **excessive consumption** (using material to fill dopamine gaps).

## 3.5 Death Mechanism: Evolution's Ultimate Weapon

### Core Proposition

**Death is not the end of life, but an efficient refresh mechanism invented by the system to resist the second law of thermodynamics (entropy increase).**

From the perspective of gene group evolution, individual death has extremely high positive significance:

### 1. Ecological Niche Vacating and Gene Renewal

- **Finiteness Axiom**: Ecological niches (resources) are limited.
- **Renewal Mechanism**: If old individuals don't die, new individuals (trial-and-error carriers of mutated genes) have no survival space.
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Death forcibly clears ecological niches, letting more adapted offspring take the field. **Without death, there is no evolution.**

### 2. Resisting Cognitive Hardening (System Heat Death)

- **Cognitive Hardening**: Over time, individual neural network paths solidify (empiricism), difficult to accept new memes.
- **System Deadlock**: If a group consists of immortal individuals, its cultural memes will stop flowing, system entropy tends to maximum (heat death).
- **Death Cleansing**: Death clears old brains hardened by path dependence, introducing "blank paper" new brains for the system.

### 3. Altruistic Self-destruction (Programmed Death)

- **Apoptosis**: For body health, damaged cells actively initiate self-destruction programs.
- **Social Death**: In tribal conflicts or resource scarcity, old and weak individuals will actively sacrifice to preserve young genes carrying more future (e.g., Inuit elder abandonment customs).
- **Conclusion**: At the group evolution level, **individual death is often a necessary cost of group survival**.
