## Core Proposition

Gene Group Evolution Theory is the **first core cornerstone** of HumanTrophicTheory, the biological origin of the entire theory, explaining the underlying logic of "where humans come from and why they are like this."

Gene Group Evolution Theory holds that the only underlying goal of life is survival and replication, and individuals are just "survival machines" made by genes to replicate themselves. The unit of evolution is not the individual, but the gene group.

## Core Axioms

### Axiom 1: The Only Underlying Goal of Life

The only underlying goal of life is **survival and replication**.

The individual is not the purpose, the continuation of the gene group is the purpose. Individuals are just "survival machines" made by genes to replicate themselves.

### Axiom 2: The Unit of Evolution is Not the Individual, but the Gene Group

Altruism, selfishness, courage, cowardice, morality, emotions... are not "character issues," but **stable strategies screened by gene groups for overall survival**.

### Axiom 3: Evolution Does Not Pursue Perfection, Only Sufficient Stability

As long as you can survive and reproduce, it is a successful strategy, regardless of good or evil, beauty or ugliness.

## Three Core Mechanisms

### Mechanism 1: Kin Selection

**Definition**: Genes will prioritize protecting relatives carrying the same gene fragments.

**Manifestations**:
- Parents' unconditional giving to children
- Family solidarity
- Blood kin loyalty

**Essence**: You protecting relatives is essentially genes protecting their own copies.

### Mechanism 2: Group Selection

**Definition**: When a group faces external threats, "self-sacrifice" behaviors will appear.

**Manifestations**:
- Warriors charging
- Acting bravely for justice
- Collectivism

**Essence**: Individual death → Group survival → Gene group continuation. Groups without this strategy will be exterminated in competition.

### Mechanism 3: Reciprocal Altruism

**Definition**: You help me, I help you, essentially a cross-time interest exchange.

**Manifestations**:
- Morality
- Reputation
- Sense of contract

**Essence**: Reducing cooperation costs, improving group survival efficiency.
