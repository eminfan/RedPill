# 5 Super Group Competition: From Zero-Sum to Non-Zero-Sum Evolutionary Game

### Core Proposition

**Competition between super groups has gradually evolved from primitive zero-sum games to non-zero-sum games, and win-win cooperation has become a higher-dimensional survival strategy.**

In the framework of gene group evolution theory, competition between different civilizations and nations is essentially the survival game of gene-culture complexes, whose forms have undergone profound evolution from violent conquest to institutional competition, from resource competition to rule-making.

---

### I. Evolutionary Hierarchy of Super Groups

#### First Level: Biological Gene Competition

- **Characteristics**: Violent expansion aimed at survival space and resource acquisition
- **Typical**: Nomadic invasion of agricultural civilizations, territorial competition in colonial era
- **Logic**: Law of the jungle, winner takes all

#### Second Level: Cultural Meme Competition

- **Characteristics**: Ideological competition centered on values and institutional output
- **Typical**: US-Soviet confrontation during Cold War, religious propagation Crusades
- **Logic**: Survival of the fittest, civilization assimilation

#### Third Level: Rule System Competition

- **Characteristics**: Institutional competition dominated by setting international rules and standards
- **Typical**: WTO rules, financial system standards, technology ethics norms
- **Logic**: Dominators set rules, sharers gain dividends

---

### II. Evolutionary Logic of Zero-Sum Games

#### Resource Scarcity Driven Zero-Sum Competition

Under resource-limited conditions, super group survival is essentially zero-sum games:

- **Land Resources**: Agricultural civilization's competition for arable land led to war and conquest
- **Energy Resources**: Industrial civilization's control over fossil fuels triggered geopolitical conflicts
- **Human Resources**: Labor mobility triggered cultural conflicts and social problems

#### Essence of Civilization Conflicts

Huntington pointed out that "clash of civilizations" is essentially the survival competition of different gene-culture complexes:

- Chinese civilization's "worldview" achieved cross-millennium survival through inclusive identity
- Roman Empire collapsed due to "citizenship meme" failure
- Islamic civilization maintained cross-regional cohesion through religious identity

---

### III. Evolutionary Path of Non-Zero-Sum Games

#### Structure from Confrontation to Cooperation

1. **Interdependence**: Industrial chain interdependence formed by economic globalization
2. **Common Threats**: Global issues such as climate warming, epidemics, AI risks
3. **Institutional Innovation**: International organizations provide cooperation platforms and rule frameworks

#### Typical Models of Non-Zero-Sum Games

**Win-Win Cooperation Type**:

- EU Integration: Member states achieve common development through institutional concessions
- Belt and Road Initiative: Infrastructure connectivity creates incremental wealth
- Carbon Trading System: Internalizes environmental costs as economic incentives

**Competition-Coexistence Type**:

- China-US Economic and Trade Relations: Bilateral relations with both competition and cooperation
- Technology Competition: Strategic competition in 5G, AI and other fields accompanied by cooperation
- Talent Flow: International circulation of intellectual resources

---

### IV. Strategy Analysis of Evolutionary Games

#### Super Group Game Strategy Matrix

|  | Cooperate | Confront |
|--|-----------|----------|
| **Cooperate** | Win-Win (Jointly Build Human Community with Shared Future) | I Win You Lose (Unilateralism) |
| **Confront** | You Win I Lose (Compromise) | Lose-Lose (Thucydides Trap) |

#### Optimal Strategy: Differentiated Coexistence

- **Avoid Homogeneous Competition**: Different civilizations leverage comparative advantages
- **Establish Competition-Cooperation Boundaries**: Compete on core interests, cooperate on public goods
- **Create Incremental Space**: Expand common interests through technological and institutional innovation

---

### V. Evolutionary Trends of Super Groups

#### 1. Transition from Violence to Institutions

- Military power's role shifts from conquest to maintaining order
- Economic strength exerts influence through rule-making
- Soft power becomes core competition dimension

#### 2. Evolution from Closed to Open

- Super group boundaries gradually blur
- Immigration and cultural exchange promote gene-culture fusion
- Transnational issues require multilateral cooperation

#### 3. Upgrade from Survival to Development

- After basic survival needs are met, development rights become main demands
- Civilization competition dimension shifts from material to cultural and institutional
- Human common destiny issues become prominent

---

### VI. Typical Cases of Contemporary Super Group Competition

#### United States: Hegemony Maintenance and Rule Dominance

- Build security networks through military alliances
- Set international economic rules through IMF, WTO and other institutions
- Export values through cultural products (Hollywood, Silicon Valley)

#### China: Peaceful Development and Common Prosperity

- Propose "Human Community with Shared Future" concept
- Build infrastructure networks through Belt and Road
- Emphasize civilization diversity, oppose civilization superiority theory

#### European Union: Supranational Governance Experiment

- Sovereignty concession achieves deep integration
- Common market creates economic efficiency
- Welfare state provides social security template

---

### Conclusion: Ultimate Direction of Evolutionary Games

The evolution of super group competition from zero-sum to non-zero-sum is the inevitable choice of gene-culture complexes to achieve fitness maximization at higher dimensions:

- **Zero-sum games** are low-dimensional survival strategies, applicable to resource-scarce eras
- **Non-zero-sum games** are high-dimensional evolutionary strategies, adapted to civilization maturity stages
- **Ultimate direction** is building a human community with shared future, achieving common development while maintaining civilization diversity

From the perspective of gene group evolution theory, the highest interest of super groups is not to eliminate opponents, but to promote the overall upgrade of human civilization through competition and cooperation.
