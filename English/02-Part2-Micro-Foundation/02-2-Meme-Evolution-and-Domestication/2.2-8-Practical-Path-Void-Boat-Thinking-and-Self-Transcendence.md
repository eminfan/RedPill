# 4 Practical Path: Void Boat Thinking and Self-Transcendence

## 4.8 Void Boat Thinking: Method of Transcending Attachments

### Core Proposition

**Void boat thinking is a thinking method that transcends individual attachments and achieves cognitive freedom.**

Void boat thinking is not passive withdrawal from the world, but a positive cognitive attitude and behavioral strategy:

### Metaphor of the Void Boat

- **Source**: The "void boat" concept in Zhuangzi's "Free and Easy Wandering"
- **Meaning**: Like an empty boat, without fixed direction and goal, drifting with the waves while maintaining inner peace
- **Essence**: Transcending individual attachments, conforming to natural laws, while maintaining inner autonomy

### Core Principles of Void Boat Thinking

1. **Non-attachment**: Not attached to specific goals, identities, or concepts
2. **Conformity**: Conforming to natural and social laws, rather than forcibly opposing
3. **Inner Stability**: Maintaining inner peace and autonomy, unaffected by external environment
4. **Flexibility**: Flexibly adjusting according to environmental changes, rather than rigid stubbornness

### Practical Methods of Void Boat Thinking

#### 1. Attachment Identification

- **Self-Observation**: Observe your thinking patterns and behavioral motives
- **Attachment Analysis**: Identify which concepts and goals are truly important, which are socially instilled
- **Value Reconstruction**: Reconstruct personal values based on real needs

#### 2. Cognitive Defusion

- **Sense of Distance**: Maintain distance from your thoughts and emotions
- **Observer Perspective**: Examine your thinking as an observer
- **Non-judgment**: Do not judge your thoughts and emotions
- **Acceptance**: Accept your thoughts and emotions, do not fight against them

#### 3. Conforming to Nature

- **Law Cognition**: Understand natural and social laws
- **Going with the Flow**: Act within the range allowed by laws
- **Resource Utilization**: Fully utilize existing resources, rather than forcibly creating
- **Rhythm Grasp**: Grasp the timing and rhythm of action

#### 4. Inner Autonomy

- **Core Values**: Clarify your core values and goals
- **Boundary Setting**: Set personal boundaries and bottom lines
- **Energy Management**: Reasonably allocate your time and energy
- **Self-Care**: Take care of your physical and mental health
