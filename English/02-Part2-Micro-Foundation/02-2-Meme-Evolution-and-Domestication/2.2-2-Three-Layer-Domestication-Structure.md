# 4 Three-Layer Domestication Structure

Meme domestication of humans is a layer-by-layer locking from emotions to identity, then to meaning, forming a complete control closed loop. This three-layer domestication structure is the core mechanism for memes to achieve comprehensive control over hosts.

## Overall Structure

```
Bottom Layer Domestication: Emotional Anchor (Trigger instinctive response)
    ↓
Middle Layer Domestication: Identity (Bind self-cognition)
    ↓
Top Layer Domestication: Meaning Assignment (Lock life purpose)
    ↓
Complete Control Closed Loop: Host actively serves memes
```

## Bottom Layer Domestication: Emotional Anchor

### Core Proposition

Memes don't persuade through reason, but directly bind to human bottom-layer emotions. Once emotions are locked, human rationality immediately goes offline, forming conditioned reflex obedience.

### Five Major Emotional Anchors

#### 1. Fear

**Mechanism**: Trigger human survival instinct by shaping threats and dangers

**Typical Memes**:
- "Not doing this will lead to destruction"
- "Enemies are hurting us"
- "If you fail, you will lose everything"

**Effect**: Fear makes people lose rational judgment ability, directly entering "fight or flight" mode

#### 2. Anger

**Mechanism**: Trigger human attack instinct by shaping injustice and enemies

**Typical Memes**:
- "They are bullying us"
- "This is righteous anger"
- "We must fight back"

**Effect**: Anger makes people lose objective judgment ability, only seeing "enemy's" mistakes, not seeing their own problems

#### 3. Guilt

**Mechanism**: Trigger human social attachment instinct by shaping "you owe others" cognition

**Typical Memes**:
- "You owe your parents"
- "You owe the collective"
- "You shouldn't be selfish"

**Effect**: Guilt makes people produce self-denial, actively obeying meme requirements to eliminate negative emotions

#### 4. Pride

**Mechanism**: Trigger human superiority instinct by granting "noble identity"

**Typical Memes**:
- "Your identity is nobler"
- "You are the chosen one"
- "We are better than them"

**Effect**: Pride makes people produce false superiority, actively maintaining memes to maintain feeling good about themselves

#### 5. Emotion

**Mechanism**: Trigger human transcendence instinct by shaping noble narratives

**Typical Memes**:
- "Sacrificing for love is noble"
- "Selfless dedication is great"
- "This is a sacred mission"

**Effect**: Emotion makes people produce moral peak experience, actively sacrificing their own interests for memes

### Operating Characteristics

- **Bypassing Reason**: Emotional reactions are much faster than rational analysis, memes directly trigger emotions
- **Immediate Effect**: Once emotions are locked, no complex persuasion process needed
- **Difficult to Resist**: Strong emotional reactions suppress rational thinking
- **Repeated Triggering**: Same emotional anchors can be used repeatedly

## Middle Layer Domestication: Identity

### Core Proposition

Memes give hosts an "exclusive identity," equating "maintaining memes" with "maintaining self." Once identity is established, questioning memes equals self-denial, thus actively obeying meme rules.

### Identity Formation Mechanism

#### Step 1: Identity Assignment

Memes directly give hosts a specific identity label:
- Nationality: You are XX nationality
- Class: You are XX class
- Belief: You are XX believer
- Gender role: You are a good man/good woman
- Group identity: You are XX fan/member

#### Step 2: Identity Internalization

Hosts transform identity labels into part of self-cognition:
- "I am an XX person" → "I am essentially an XX person"
- "I identify with XX" → "I naturally believe XX"

#### Step 3: Identity Binding

Equate "maintaining identity" with "maintaining self":
- Questioning memes = Questioning my identity = Questioning my self
- Betraying memes = Betraying my identity = Betraying myself

### Identity Control Effects

#### 1. Self-Defense

When memes are questioned, hosts' first reaction is self-defense:
- "You criticize XX, you are criticizing me"
- "You deny XX, you are denying my life"
- Anger, rejection, hostility, directly closing rational channels

#### 2. Active Maintenance

Hosts will actively maintain memes, because maintaining memes is maintaining self:
- Actively propagate memes
- Actively attack dissenters
- Actively reject "people not like us"

#### 3. Group Pressure

Identity produces powerful group pressure:
- "Real XX wouldn't do this"
- "XX should be like this"
- Behaviors not conforming to identity norms will be rejected by the group

### Typical Cases

| Meme Type | Identity Label | Identity Norm |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| Nationalism | Chinese | Must be patriotic, must struggle for national rise |
| Religion | Believer | Must be devout, must observe doctrine, must propagate faith |
| Gender Role | Good man/good woman | Must have responsibility, must support family, must be loyal |
| Consumerism | Middle class | Must have taste, must upgrade consumption, must show status |
| Workplace Culture | Striver | Must work overtime, must struggle, must grow with company |

## Top Layer Domestication: Meaning Assignment

### Core Proposition

Humans naturally fear meaninglessness, memes implant "false purposes" into hosts' lives, using meaningless meaning to completely lock people in. Hosts voluntarily sacrifice their own interests, even lives, for memes.

### Meaning Assignment Mechanism

#### 1. False Purpose Implantation

Memes give people a life purpose transcending the individual:
- Religion: To enter heaven, obtain eternal salvation
- Ideology: To liberate all humanity, realize historical inevitability
- Nationalism: For national rejuvenation, national rise
- Consumerism: For a better life, become a life winner
- Love: For true love, for that person

#### 2. Sacralization Packaging

Sanctify ordinary behaviors as "noble missions":
- Work → Struggle
- Consumption → Loving yourself
- Reproduction → Inheritance
- Sacrifice → Glory

#### 3. Meaning Binding Life

Bind meme purposes with individual life value:
- Questioning purpose = Questioning whether my life has meaning
- Giving up purpose = Giving up my life value
- Betraying purpose = I am a worthless person

### Meaning Assignment Control Effects

#### 1. Transcending Pros and Cons Calculation

Sacred meaning makes people unable to perform rational pros and cons calculation:
- "You spend so much money and time, it's not worth it" → "You don't understand at all, this is my faith"
- "If you die, everything is gone" → "For eternal happiness, sacrifice is worth it"

#### 2. Sacrificing Self-Interest

Hosts voluntarily sacrifice their own interests for "sacred purposes":
- Give up personal happiness
- Consume personal wealth
- Even give up lives

#### 3. Active Propagation

Hosts believe propagating memes is "saving others":
- "I want more people to understand this truth"
- "This is for your own good"
- Forced propagation, moral kidnapping

### Typical Cases

| False Purpose | Sacred Packaging | Sacrifice Manifestation |
|--------------|------------------|------------------------|
| Enter heaven | Eternal salvation | Martyrdom, celibacy, giving up secular life |
| National rise | Historical mission | Dying for country, sacrificing personal happiness |
| Idol chasing | Guarding idol | Fundraising and voting, abandoning studies and work |
| Buying house | Life success | Emptying six wallets, thirty-year mortgage |
| Entrepreneurship | Changing the world | 996, giving up health and family |

## Synergistic Operation of Three-Layer Domestication

### Complete Control Closed Loop

```
Emotional Anchor (Bottom Layer Locking)
    ↓
Emotions bound by specific memes
    ↓
Identity (Middle Layer Binding)
    ↓
Maintaining memes = Maintaining self
    ↓
Meaning Assignment (Top Layer Locking)
    ↓
Sacrificing for memes = Life has value
    ↓
Host becomes permanent host of memes
```

### Progressive Relationship

1. **Bottom Layer First**: Emotional anchors are the fastest control method, directly triggering instinctive responses
2. **Middle Layer Reinforcement**: Identity binds memes with self, forming more stable control
3. **Top Layer Locking**: Meaning assignment gives life purpose, fundamentally eliminating resistance possibility

### Reasons Difficult to Break Through

- Break through bottom layer: Emotional reactions are fast and strong, difficult to rationally control
- Break through middle layer: Identity has been internalized as part of self-cognition, questioning memes = questioning self
- Break through top layer: Meaning has been bound with life value, giving up meaning = giving up life value

## Practical Application

### Identifying Three-Layer Domestication

1. **Observe emotional reactions**: Are you triggered by specific emotions? Is it difficult to think rationally?
2. **Observe identity labels**: Do you equate "maintaining something" with "maintaining self"?
3. **Observe meaning binding**: Have you sacrificed your own interests for some "noble purpose"?

### Path to Break Through Domestication

1. **Emotional Decoupling**: Identify emotional trigger mechanisms, don't be led by emotions
2. **Identity Separation**: Separate "me" from "my identity labels"
3. **Meaning Independence**: Establish life meaning independent of memes
