## Core Definitions

### 1. Meme

A meme is **a gene at the cultural level**, any information unit that can replicate, propagate, and mutate in human brains:
- A sentence, a concept, a belief, an aesthetic
- A set of moral norms, an identity
- A political slogan, a success doctrine, a view of love

**Core Characteristics**:
- Genes control human bodies and biological instincts, memes control human thoughts, emotions, and behaviors
- The only ultimate goal of memes: self-replication and propagation
- Memes don't care about the host's happiness, health, or even life and death, only whether they can occupy more human brains
- Humans are hosts of memes, often "you think you are thinking, but actually memes are thinking through your brain"

### 2. Domestication

Domestication is the core function of memes, referring to **memes hijacking the host's judgment, emotions, and behaviors through long-term implantation, making them serve meme propagation and social energy flow stability, rather than the host's own core interests**.

## Three Laws of Meme Evolution

Memes follow evolutionary logic similar to genes, with three inviolable underlying laws:

### Law 1: Transmission Priority Over Truth

Meme survival does not depend on whether it conforms to facts, only on transmission ability. Memes that can trigger strong emotions (anger, fear, guilt, superiority), are simple and easy to understand, and conform to group psychology will always propagate faster and survive longer than rigorous, complex, neutral facts.

**Typical Cases**: Conspiracy theories > rigorous facts, emotional slogans > logical analysis, moral judgment > rational discussion

### Law 2: Memes Will Cluster to Form "Meme Complexes"

Single memes have extremely weak control power, multiple complementary memes combined will form an extremely strong control closed loop. Typical complex structure:
- Identity + Moral Justice + Enemy Image + Sacred Mission
- Finally forming strong control systems such as religions, ideologies, fan circles, extreme nationalism

### Law 3: Memes Will Evolve "Self-Protection Mechanisms"

Mature domestication memes all come with defense systems, making hosts dare not question and cannot leave:
- Questioning it = immoral/betrayal/great treason
- Leaving it = pain/anxiety/guilt
- Finally making hosts actively maintain memes, even attack dissenters

## Three-Layer Complete Structure of Meme Domestication

Meme domestication of humans is a layer-by-layer locking from emotions to identity, then to meaning, forming a complete control closed loop:

### Bottom Layer Domestication: Emotional Anchor

Memes don't persuade through reason, but directly bind to human bottom-layer emotions:
- Fear (not doing this will lead to destruction)
- Anger (enemies are hurting us)
- Guilt (you owe the collective/others)
- Pride (your identity is nobler)
- Emotion (sacrificing for love is noble)

**Effect**: Once emotions are locked, human rationality immediately goes offline, forming conditioned reflex obedience.

### Middle Layer Domestication: Identity

Memes give hosts an "exclusive identity":
- You are XX nationality, XX class, XX believer
- You are a good man/good woman, XX fan

**Effect**: Once identity is established, hosts equate "maintaining memes" with "maintaining self," questioning memes equals self-denial, thus actively obeying meme rules.

### Top Layer Domestication: Meaning Assignment

Humans naturally fear meaninglessness, memes implant "false purposes" into hosts' lives:
- Sacrificing for faith, struggling for the collective
- Burning for love, desperately for success
- Dedicating for country

**Effect**: Using meaningless meaning to completely lock people in, making hosts voluntarily sacrifice their own interests, even lives, for memes.

## Five Major Domestication Systems

### 1. Moral Domestication Memes

**Core Function**: Reduce governance costs, stabilize bottom-layer order

**Typical Content**: Hard work leads to wealth, good people get good rewards, suffering is a blessing, loyalty and filial piety, patience and non-contention

**Essence**: Let the bottom layer willingly be harvested, not resist, not question, not seek class transition

### 2. Love Domestication Memes

**Typical Content**: One life one world one person, true love is unique, sacrifice for love, marriage is sacred responsibility

**Essence**: Use emotional shackles to force long-term cooperative child-rearing between genders, serving gene continuation and social structure stability

### 3. Consumption Domestication Memes

**Core Function**: Stimulate desires, create involution, harvest wealth

**Typical Content**: You deserve better, loving yourself means buying, taste = class, not buying is failure

**Essence**: Humans work for goods, lifelong debt, their own energy continuously extracted

**Core Function**: Give the bottom layer hope, prevent system collapse

**Typical Content**: As long as you work hard you can reverse destiny, you're not successful because you're not self-disciplined, poor families produce noble sons

**Essence**: Social trophic level structure determines the vast majority cannot transition, success doctrine is just a placebo meme appeasing the bottom layer

### 5. Collective Domestication Memes

**Core Function**: Drive individuals to sacrifice for the group, maintain super group competitive advantage

**Typical Content**: Patriotism, collective honor, mission calling, sacrifice small self to complete big self

**Essence**: Maximally extract individual energy to the group, is the most efficient energy harvesting mechanism

Once a meme complex forms, it is almost impossible to be defeated by reason. Reason and meme complexes are not on the same "attack dimension" at all.

### Mechanism 1: Self-Binding

Meme complexes completely bind "memes" with "your self." Denying memes = denying my self-existence, questioning memes = questioning the meaning of my life.

### Mechanism 2: Moral Original Sin

Meme complexes set a moral death penalty for "doubt": questioning = immoral/betrayal/great treason. Humans naturally avoid moral stains, so they dare not initiate reason at all.

### Mechanism 3: Information Cocoon

### Mechanism 4: Meaning Crushing

Meme complexes give people sacred meaning that transcends life and worldly pros and cons, reason cannot calculate its "pros and cons."

### Mechanism 5: Backfire Effect

The more you use reason to refute meme complexes, the stronger they become.
