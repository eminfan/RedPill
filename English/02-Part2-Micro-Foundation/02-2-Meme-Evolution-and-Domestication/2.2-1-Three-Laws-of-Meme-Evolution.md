# 4 Three Laws of Meme Evolution

Memes follow evolutionary logic similar to genes, with three inviolable underlying laws. These three laws explain how memes propagate, evolve, and solidify in human society.

## Law 1: Transmission Priority Over Truth

### Core Proposition

Meme survival does not depend on whether it conforms to facts, only on transmission ability.

### Operating Mechanism

Memes that can trigger strong emotions, are simple and easy to understand, and conform to group psychology will always propagate faster and survive longer than rigorous, complex, neutral facts.

**Core Principle**: Human brains default to using "fast thinking" mode (heuristic processing), and only through deliberate training can they use "slow thinking" mode (analytical processing). Memes exploit this cognitive characteristic to directly trigger emotional responses, bypassing rational analysis.

### Typical Cases

| Low Transmission Content | High Transmission Content |
|-------------------------|--------------------------|
| Rigorous factual analysis | Emotionally charged conspiracy theories |
| Complex logical reasoning | Simple and powerful emotional slogans |
| Objective and neutral data | Moral judgment and position expression |

### Inferences

- **Facts do not equal truth**: Memes that conform to facts may not propagate
- **Emotion is the fuel of transmission**: Strong emotions such as anger, fear, and guilt can greatly improve transmission efficiency
- **Simple overwhelms complex**: Simple and crude narratives always propagate more widely than precise and complex arguments
- **Group psychology is the breeding ground**: Content that conforms to biases, prejudices, and group identity propagates more easily

## Law 2: Memes Will Cluster to Form "Meme Complexes"

### Core Proposition

Single memes have extremely weak control power, multiple complementary memes combined will form an extremely strong control closed loop.

### Typical Structure of Meme Complexes

```
Identity + Moral Justice + Enemy Image + Sacred Mission
     ↓           ↓          ↓          ↓
  "I am XX"   "XX is just"  "Enemy is destroying"  "Fight for XX"
```

### Core Characteristics of Meme Complexes

1. **Complementary Enhancement**: Each meme strengthens other memes' control power
2. **Closed Loop Locking**: Complete structure, cannot be broken through from inside
3. **Self-Reinforcement**: Hosts will actively maintain and propagate the complex
4. **Exclusivity**: Strong rejection and aggression toward dissenters

### Typical Cases

| Meme Complex | Constituent Elements |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Extreme Religion | Identity (believer) + Justice (sacred doctrine) + Enemy (infidels) + Mission (holy war) |
| Extreme Nationalism | Identity (nation) + Justice (patriotism) + Enemy (foreign forces) + Mission (rise) |
| Fan Circle Culture | Identity (fan) + Justice (idol is best) + Enemy (haters) + Mission (guard) |
| Ideology | Identity (class) + Justice (historical inevitability) + Enemy (reactionaries) + Mission (liberation) |

### Inferences

- **Complex > Single Meme**: The control power of complexes far exceeds the sum of single memes
- **Dismantling needs to be from inside**: Attacking complexes from outside will instead strengthen their internal cohesion
- **Beware of perfect narratives**: When a narrative is "flawless," it is often a signal of a meme complex

## Law 3: Memes Will Evolve "Self-Protection Mechanisms"

### Core Proposition

Mature domestication memes all come with defense systems, making hosts dare not question and cannot leave.

### Types of Self-Protection Mechanisms

#### 1. Moral Binding

Define "questioning memes" as "immoral" behavior:
- Religion: Not believing, questioning god, is the greatest sin
- Ideology: Questioning the concept, is a traitor, counter-revolutionary
- Collectivism: Doubting the collective, is selfish
- Fan circle: Leaving fans and criticizing back, is shameful

**Effect**: Questioners are directly morally judged, cannot obtain group identity

#### 2. Pain Binding

Forcefully associate "leaving memes" with "painful experience":
- Losing faith = losing life meaning
- Betraying the collective = being abandoned by all
- Leaving fans = emptiness and loneliness
- Questioning authority = existential crisis

**Effect**: Leaving memes will trigger strong negative emotions, forming psychological withdrawal reactions

#### 3. Enemy Narrative

Define "counter-evidence information" as "enemy conspiracy":
- Official clarification = officials have been bought
- Objective data = data has been forged
- Rational analysis = being brainwashed
- Different opinions = sent by enemies

**Effect**: Cut off external information sources, forming information cocoons

#### 4. Backfire Effect

Even successfully letting hosts contact counter-evidence information will instead strengthen their faith in original memes.

**Psychological Mechanism**: When core beliefs are threatened, people resist the threat by strengthening beliefs

**Effect**: Rationally attacking meme complexes will only make them more solid

### Synergistic Operation of Three Laws

```
Transmission Priority (Diffusion)
        ↓
    Meme Complex Formation (Solidification)
        ↓
    Self-Protection Mechanism (Defense)
        ↓
    Immune to Rational Attack (Survival)
```

These three laws together constitute the core logic of meme evolution, explaining why domestication memes are so difficult to break.

## Practical Application

### Identifying Meme Complexes

1. Observe if there is a "perfect narrative" — unfalsifiable, impeccable
2. Observe if there is strong exclusivity — no questioning, no criticism allowed
3. Observe if there is an "enemy image" — need to constantly fight imaginary enemies
4. Observe if there is a "sacred mission" — sacred goals transcending personal interests

### Resisting Meme Domestication

1. Identify meme transmission mechanisms, don't be emotionally manipulated
2. Be alert to meme complex formation, avoid being locked by single narratives
3. Maintain independent thinking, don't be kidnapped by self-protection mechanisms
