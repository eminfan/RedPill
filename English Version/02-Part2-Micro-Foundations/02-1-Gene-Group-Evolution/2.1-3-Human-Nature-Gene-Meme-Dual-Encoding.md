# 3 Human Nature: Gene-Meme Dual Encoding

## 3.1 Dual Encoding Essence: Gene-Culture Complex

### Core Proposition

**Humans are life continuation systems jointly constituted and co-evolving by genes and cultural memes.**

This proposition subverts the traditional view of human nature, viewing humans as a complex system of dual encoding:

### Dual Encoding Structure

#### Gene Layer: Underlying Biological Instincts

- **Function**: Sets underlying biological instincts (five desires), provides the system's energy supply and collaboration prototype
- **Evolution Speed**: Extremely slow, measured in tens of thousands of years
- **Core Mechanism**: Optimizes gene frequency through natural selection
- **Manifestations**: Physiological instincts, emotional responses, basic behavioral patterns

#### Meme Layer: Upper Social Rules

- **Function**: Constructs super-biological adaptation systems (rules, institutions, culture), expands survival strategy space
- **Evolution Speed**: Extremely fast, measured in centuries, decades, or even years
- **Core Mechanism**: Optimizes meme transmission through cultural selection
- **Manifestations**: Language, morality, law, institutions, technology

### Synergy and Conflict: Battlefield Model

The relationship between genes and memes is not just "synergy," but also "gaming." The human body is the **host and battlefield** of these two replicators:

1. **Synergy (Symbiosis)**: Memes help genes survive better (e.g., agricultural technology memes caused human population to surge).
2. **Conflict (Parasitism)**: Memes, for their own propagation, may sacrifice gene interests (e.g., celibacy, martyrdom, excessive overtime).
    * *Note: When a meme can make people sacrifice reproductive opportunities to propagate it, that meme has defeated the gene.*

## 3.2 Extended Phenotype Principle: Biological Basis of Social Systems

### Core Proposition

**Social systems are "extended bodies" constructed by gene groups at the cultural level to maximize their own fitness.**

This principle reveals the essence of social systems:

### Concept of Extended Phenotype

- **Biological Origin**: Proposed by Dawkins in *The Selfish Gene*, referring to gene phenotypic expression extending beyond individual body boundaries
- **Classic Examples**: Spider webs, beaver dams, bird nests
- **Human Extension**: Human social systems are extended phenotypes of genes, the "collective body" of gene groups

### Extended Phenotype Characteristics of Social Systems

1. **Functional Service**: Social systems serve the survival and reproduction of gene groups
2. **Hereditary Transmission**: Social systems are transmitted through memes, achieving the continued existence of gene groups
3. **Adaptive Evolution**: Social systems evolve with environmental changes, improving gene group fitness

### Theoretical Significance

- **Breaking Binary Opposition**: Eliminates the "biology-culture" binary opposition, viewing social systems as natural extensions of biological evolution
- **Unified Explanatory Framework**: Explaining the origin and function of social phenomena from the gene level
- **Predictive Capability**: Predicting the evolutionary direction of social systems based on gene groups' survival needs

## 3.3 Negative Entropy Drive Logic: The System's Energy Demand

### Core Proposition

**The more ordered the system, the greater the negative entropy flow required. The edifice of human civilization is built upon the continuous extraction of individual biological energy.**

### Thermodynamic Explanation: The Price of Order

According to dissipative structure theory:
$$ \Delta S_{total} = \Delta S_{system} + \Delta S_{environment} > 0 $$

- To maintain the **social system (System)** in a low-entropy (ordered, stable) state.
- More entropy must be discharged into the **environment (Environment)**.
- In sociology, **individuals often become the "environment" that absorbs entropy**.

**Manifestations**:
- **996 Work System**: The system gains orderly profits (low entropy), individuals gain disordered bodies and minds (high entropy/overwork).
- **War**: The nation gains secure boundaries (order), soldiers gain death and disability (maximum entropy).

## 3.4 Three Derivative Mechanisms: Dynamic Gaming of Dual Encoding

The interaction between genes and memes within the human body derives three basic social behavior patterns:

### Mechanism One: Meme Suppression (Superego vs. Id)

- **Definition**: Memes forcibly suppress gene instincts to maintain group order.
- **Cases**:
    - **Chastity concepts** (meme) suppress **sexual desire** (gene).
    - **Anti-corruption education** (meme) suppresses **greed** (gene).
- **Cost**: Individuals experience psychological conflict, neurosis, repression (Freud's "discontent of civilization").

### Mechanism Two: Gene Rebellion (Instinct Breakthrough)

- **Definition**: Gene instincts break through meme constraints, causing social rules to fail.
- **Cases**:
    - **Exchanging children to eat** during famine (survival instinct pierces moral memes).
    - **Extramarital affairs** under strict monogamy (reproductive strategy pierces legal memes).
- **Result**: Social disorder, system entropy increase.

### Mechanism Three: False Complicity (Sanctification)

- **Definition**: Memes exploit gene reward mechanisms (dopamine) to induce individuals to sacrifice for the group.
- **Mechanism**:
    - Genes set the "seeking recognition" reward circuit.
    - Memes define "sacrifice is glorious."
    - Result: Individuals receive massive dopamine rewards (sublimity) when sacrificing; genes think they've won (gained status), memes also win (gained propagation).
- **Cases**: **Religious martyrdom**, **suicide attacks**, **excessive consumption** (filling dopamine gaps with material goods).

## 3.5 Death Mechanism: Evolution's Ultimate Weapon

### Core Proposition

**Death is not the end of life, but an efficient refresh mechanism invented by the system to counter the Second Law of Thermodynamics (entropy increase).**

From the perspective of gene group evolution, individual death has extremely high positive significance:

### 1. Ecological Niche Vacating and Gene Renewal

- **Finiteness Axiom**: Ecological niches (resources) are finite.
- **Renewal Mechanism**: If old individuals don't die, new individuals (trial-and-error carriers of mutated genes) have no living space.
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Death forcibly clears ecological niches, allowing better-adapted offspring to take the stage. **Without death, there is no evolution.**

### 2. Countering Cognitive Hardening (System Heat Death)

- **Cognitive Hardening**: Over time, individual neural network pathways solidify (empiricism), making it difficult to accept new memes.
- **System Stagnation**: If a group consisted of immortal individuals, its cultural memes would stop flowing, and system entropy would approach maximum (heat death).
- **Death Cleansing**: Death clears old brains rigidified by path dependency, introducing "blank slate" new brains to the system.

### 3. Altruistic Self-Destruction (Programmed Death)

- **Apoptosis**: For the health of the organism, damaged cells actively initiate self-destruction programs.
- **Social Death**: In tribal conflicts or resource scarcity, the old and weak actively sacrifice themselves to preserve the young who carry more future (e.g., Inuit elder abandonment customs).
- **Conclusion**: At the group evolution level, **individual death is often a necessary cost for group survival**.
