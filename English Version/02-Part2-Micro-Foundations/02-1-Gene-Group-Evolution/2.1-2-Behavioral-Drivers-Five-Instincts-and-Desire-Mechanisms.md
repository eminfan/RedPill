# 4 Behavioral Drivers: Five Instincts and Desire Mechanisms

## 4.4 Dynamic Balance of Desires

## 4.1 Five Instinctive Desire Programming

### Core Proposition

**The underlying driving force of human behavior is five instinctive desires, which are the core survival algorithms of gene programming.**

These instinctive desires are not simple physiological needs, but complex behavioral programs designed by gene groups for their own survival:

### Five Instinctive Desires

#### 1. Survival Desire

- **Core Function**: Maintain carrier endurance
- **Manifestations**: Hunger, thirst, fear of danger, self-protection
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Ensures individual survival, providing carriers for gene transmission
- **Social Impact**: Drives individuals to acquire basic survival resources; the foundation of economic activity

#### 2. Reproduction Desire

- **Core Function**: Core gene replication program
- **Manifestations**: Sexual desire, courtship behavior, nurturing instinct, parent-child attachment
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Achieves direct gene transmission; key to population continuation
- **Social Impact**: Shapes family structure, gender relations, marriage systems

#### 3. Social Desire

- **Core Function**: Construct a "group safety net"
- **Manifestations**: Sense of belonging, sense of identity, social needs, group identification
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Improves survival probability through group collaboration; foundation of social formation
- **Social Impact**: Drives individuals to integrate into groups, forming social organizations

#### 4. Development Desire

- **Core Function**: Environmental pre-adaptation mechanism
- **Manifestations**: Learning skills, resource monopolization, power competition, status pursuit
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Improves individual and group adaptability, responding to environmental changes
- **Social Impact**: Promotes technological progress, social stratification, civilizational development

#### 5. Pleasure Desire

- **Core Function**: Behavioral reward system
- **Manifestations**: Chemical signals such as dopamine and endorphins; pursuit of pleasurable experiences
- **Evolutionary Significance**: Reinforces behaviors beneficial to survival and reproduction
- **Social Impact**: Drives consumer behavior, entertainment industry, cultural creation

### Hierarchical Structure of Desires

The five desires do not exist in isolation, but form a hierarchical structure:

1. **Foundation Layer**: Survival desire (most basic, foundation for other desires)
2. **Core Layer**: Reproduction desire (direct driver of gene transmission)
3. **Expansion Layer**: Social desire (need for group collaboration)
4. **Elevation Layer**: Development desire (enhancement of adaptive capacity)
5. **Feedback Layer**: Pleasure desire (reward mechanism for behavior)

### Dynamic Balance of Desires

- **Mutual Constraint**: Satisfaction of one desire affects the intensity of other desires
- **Dynamic Adjustment**: Based on environmental changes, the priority of desires automatically adjusts
- **Individual Differences**: Different individuals have different desire intensities and priorities

## 4.2 Emotional Feedback Loop

### Core Proposition

**Emotions are feedback mechanisms designed by genes to regulate individual behavior and ensure group interests.**

Emotions are not random subjective feelings, but behavioral regulation systems programmed by genes:

### Positive Emotions: Reward Mechanism

- **Love and Belonging**: Neural reward from genes for behaviors beneficial to group collaboration
- **Achievement**: Positive feedback for individual contributions
- **Pleasure**: Direct reward for behaviors beneficial to survival and reproduction
- **Trust**: Emotional mechanism promoting intra-group cooperation

### Negative Emotions: Warning Mechanism

- **Fear**: Warning of threats to survival
- **Shame**: Punishment for behaviors damaging group interests
- **Anxiety**: Warning of potential risks, driving individuals to take action
- **Jealousy**: Perception of unfair resource distribution, protecting individual rights

### Social Functions of Emotions

1. **Behavior Regulation**: Guiding individual behavior through emotional experience
2. **Group Coordination**: Emotional resonance promotes behavioral synchronization among group members
3. **Information Transmission**: Emotional expression transmits individual state and need information
4. **Social Control**: Maintaining social norms through emotional pressure

## 4.3 Evolutionary Logic of Behavior

### Core Proposition

**The ultimate driving force of human behavior is the survival and reproduction of gene groups; individual behavior is a tool for gene groups to achieve this goal.**

### Evolutionary Mechanism of Behavior

1. **Variation**: Diversity of individual behavior provides material for selection
2. **Selection**: Behaviors beneficial to gene group survival are preserved
3. **Inheritance**: Beneficial behaviors are transmitted to offspring through genes and memes

### Evolutionary Analysis of Behavior Types

#### Altruistic Behavior

- **Kin Altruism**: Altruistic behavior based on gene similarity
- **Reciprocal Altruism**: Altruistic behavior based on long-term interest exchange
- **Group Altruism**: Altruistic behavior beneficial to the entire group

#### Selfish Behavior

- **Reasonable Self-Interest**: Behavior beneficial to individual survival and reproduction
- **Extreme Selfishness**: Selfish behavior that damages group interests
- **Evolutionary Balance**: Dynamic balance between selfish and altruistic behavior

### Cultural Shaping of Behavior

- **Meme Filtering**: Cultural rules screen and shape individual behavior
- **Social Norms**: Guiding behavior through rewards and punishments
- **Values**: Shaping the goals and meaning of behavior
