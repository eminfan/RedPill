## Part Six: Anti-Domestication Five-Step Method

### Step One: Cognitive Audit—Identify What's Controlling You

**Operational Method**:

1. List your 5-10 most firmly held core beliefs
2. For each belief, ask three questions:
   - Where did this belief come from? (Family? Education? Media? Social interaction?)
   - Whose interests does it serve? (Mine? Some group? Some industry?)
   - What does questioning it make me fear? (Being negated? Being excluded? Losing meaning?)
3. Identify the meme complex structure behind each belief
4. Assess each belief's "autonomy index" (1-10, 10 = completely autonomous choice)

### Step Two: Emotional Unbinding—Distinguish Genuine Emotions from Implanted Emotions

**Operational Method**:

1. Establish an "emotion pause" habit: When strong emotions arise, pause for 3 seconds first
2. Activate "emotion audit":
   - Is this emotion automatically triggered or after deliberation?
   - Which genetic instinct does this emotion interface with?
   - Whose interests does this emotion serve?
3. After seeing the emotion's source, autonomously choose how to respond
4. Practice continuously, gradually shortening the "trigger → awareness → choice" time

### Step Three: Framework Reconstruction—Break Semantic Anchoring, Build Multiple Frameworks

**Operational Method**:

1. List your definitions of core concepts (success, happiness, freedom, value, responsibility)
2. Deliberately find at least 3 different interpretations for each concept
3. Practice switching between different frameworks
4. When facing problems, deliberately analyze using different frameworks, finding the interpretation most suitable for yourself
5. Don't be locked into any single framework; maintain framework flexibility

### Step Four: Energy Recovery—Stop Providing Computing Power for Predatory Meme Groups

**Operational Method**:

1. Audit your time and energy allocation:
   - What do you spend the most time on each day?
   - Which meme group's interests does each activity serve?
   - What is the real value of each activity to "me"?
2. Identify "computing power leakage":
   - Unconscious anxiety consumption
   - Social consumption driven by group pressure
   - Attention consumption driven by consumer desire
3. Recover computing power:
   - Reduce or stop providing computing power for predatory meme groups
   - Redirect recovered computing power toward autonomously chosen directions
4. Establish an "energy budget": Allocate a fixed proportion of energy daily to autonomously chosen activities

### Step Five: Active Choice—After Seeing the Truth, Still Choose to Live Awake

**Operational Method**:

1. Identify: Which memes are controlling me right now?
2. Examine: What are the control mechanisms of these memes?
3. Evaluate: Are these memes beneficial or harmful to me?
4. Choose:
   - Retain memes beneficial to me (such as basic moral norms, effective collaboration rules)
   - Abandon memes harmful to me (such as anxiety-driven success ideology, fear-driven consumerism)
   - Find better alternatives (such as from "success = wealth" to "success = self-actualization")
