# Five Theorems of Dual-Group Evolution

## Core Proposition

Individuals are dual hosts of gene groups and meme groups. The five theorems of dual-group evolution reveal how gene groups and meme groups jointly shape individual behavior, how they game against each other, and how they determine individual destiny.

---

## Theorem One: Individual Dual-Host Attribute Theorem

### Core Content

Individuals innately possess dual-host attributes, serving as dual connection carriers for gene groups and meme groups. Gene groups write underlying instinct encoding through heredity; meme groups pre-install cognitive operating systems through socialization.

### Dual Encoding Mechanism

| Encoding Source | Encoding Method | Encoding Content | Encoding Level |
|----------------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Gene groups | Heredity | Survival instincts, reproduction instincts, group belonging instincts | Biological foundation |
| Meme groups | Socialization | Values, identity, behavioral norms | Cultural surface |

### Synergy of Dual Encoding

Gene encoding provides "hardware drivers"; meme encoding provides "software programs":

```
Gene Encoding (Hardware Drivers)
  ├─ Survival instinct → Exploited by success ideology memes
  ├─ Reproduction instinct → Exploited by romantic love memes
  ├─ Group belonging instinct → Exploited by collectivist memes
  └─ Status competition instinct → Exploited by consumerist memes

Meme Encoding (Software Programs)
  ├─ Values → Define what is "good"
  ├─ Identity → Define "who I am"
  └─ Behavioral norms → Define "what I should do"
```

### Deep Implications of the Theorem

Individual behavior is not the product of "free will," but the output of dual encoding. What is called the "self" is largely the running interface of dual-group encoding.

## Theorem Two: Dual-Group Interest Conflict Theorem

### Core Content

The interests of gene groups (individual survival and reproduction) and the interests of meme groups (meme replication and propagation) have fundamental conflicts. Meme groups may require hosts to sacrifice gene interests to serve meme propagation.

### Manifestations of Interest Conflict

| Conflict Type | Gene Interest | Meme Interest | Typical Case |
|--------------|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| Survival sacrifice | Individual survival | Meme propagation | Suicide attacks, martyrdom |
| Reproduction sacrifice | Gene transmission | Meme purity | Celibacy, asceticism |
| Resource sacrifice | Individual resource accumulation | Meme group resources | Excessive dedication, being harvested |
| Time sacrifice | Individual life time | Meme propagation time | Devoting an entire life to faith |

### Judgment Rules for Conflict

When a behavior meets the following conditions, it indicates the meme group is sacrificing the host's gene interests:

1. The behavior damages individual survival (health, safety, lifespan)
2. The behavior damages individual reproduction (reproductive opportunities, offspring investment)
3. The behavior consumes individual resources (time, energy, wealth)
4. The behavior primarily serves meme propagation rather than individual interests

### Deep Implications of the Theorem

Meme groups don't care about the host's happiness, health, or even life and death; they only care about meme replication and propagation. Individuals may be "kidnapped" by memes, becoming meme puppets.

## Theorem Three: Meme Shell-Riding Expression of Genes Theorem

### Core Content

Meme groups achieve shell-riding expression of memes by interfacing with the underlying instincts of gene groups. Memes don't directly drive behavior; they drive behavior by activating genetic instincts.

### Shell-Riding Expression Mechanism

```
Meme (Cultural Instruction)
  ↓ Interface
Genetic Instinct (Underlying Drive)
  ↓ Activate
Behavioral Output
```

### Typical Shell-Riding Methods

| Meme Type | Interfaced Genetic Instinct | Shell-Riding Expression Method |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Success ideology memes | Status competition instinct | "Success = high status, failure = low status" |
| Romantic love memes | Reproduction instinct, intimacy needs | "True love = the highest emotion" |
| Consumerist memes | Resource acquisition instinct, status competition instinct | "Consumption = acquiring resources + displaying status" |
| Collectivist memes | Group belonging instinct | "Obeying the collective = gaining belonging" |
| Moral memes | Reciprocal altruism instinct | "Being a good person = gaining group approval" |

### Deep Implications of the Theorem

The power of memes comes from interfacing with genetic instincts. The more precisely a meme can interface with genetic instincts, the stronger its transmission power and control power.

## Theorem Five: Meme Group-Level Integration Theorem

### Core Content

Meme groups form super-individuals at the group scale, possessing integration capabilities that transcend any single individual. Super-individuals achieve integration of the group through three major mechanisms: group crisis activation, consensus framework solidification, and self-purification with boundary maintenance.

### Three Major Integration Mechanisms

#### Mechanism One: Group Crisis Activation

When the group faces external threats, the super-individual's integration degree sharply increases, individual differences are compressed, and group behavior becomes highly unified.

#### Mechanism Two: Consensus Framework Solidification

After group consensus forms, the super-individual upgrades the integration state from "temporary emergency" to "long-term stability" by solidifying the consensus framework.

#### Mechanism Three: Self-Purification and Boundary Maintenance

The super-individual ensures internal unity and clear external boundaries through continuous self-purification and boundary maintenance.

### Characteristics of Super-Individuals

| Characteristic | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Super-individual intelligence | Group-level intelligence transcending any single individual |
| Super-individual will | Group-level will transcending any single individual |
| Super-individual behavior | Group-level behavior transcending any single individual |
| Integration capability | Integrating countless individuals into a coordinately operating whole |

### Deep Implications of the Theorem

Individuals are not only controlled by memes but also integrated by the super-individual formed by meme groups. Individual behavior, emotions, and opinions all provide "computing power" for the super-individual's operation.
