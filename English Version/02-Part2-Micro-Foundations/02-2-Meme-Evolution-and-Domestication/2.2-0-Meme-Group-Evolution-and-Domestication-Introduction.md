A meme is a **gene at the cultural level**, any information unit that can replicate, propagate, and mutate in human brains:
- A sentence, a concept, a belief, an aesthetic
- A set of moral norms, an identity

- The sole ultimate goal of memes: self-replication and propagation
- Memes do not care about the host's happiness, health, or even life and death; they only care about whether they can occupy more brains

## Three Laws of Meme Evolution

Memes follow evolutionary logic similar to genes, with three inviolable underlying laws:

### Law One: Transmission Priority Over Truth

Meme survival does not depend on whether it conforms to facts, only on its transmission capability. Memes that can trigger strong emotions (anger, fear, guilt, superiority), are simple and easy to understand, and conform to group psychology will always propagate faster and survive longer than rigorous, complex, neutral facts.

**Typical Cases**: Conspiracy theories > rigorous facts, emotional slogans > logical analysis, moral judgment > rational discussion

### Law Two: Memes Cluster to Form "Meme Complexes"

A single meme has very weak control power; multiple complementary memes combine to form an extremely strong control closed loop. Typical complex structure:
- Identity + Moral Justice + Enemy Image + Sacred Mission
- Ultimately forming strong control systems such as religions, ideologies, fan circles, extreme nationalism

### Law Three: Memes Evolve "Self-Protection Mechanisms"

Mature domestication memes all come with built-in defense systems, making hosts afraid to question and unable to leave:
- Questioning it = immoral/betrayal/treason
- Leaving it = pain/anxiety/guilt
- Ultimately making hosts actively maintain memes and even attack dissenters

The core logic of all rules is consistent: **the ultimate goal of meme groups is always their own cross-generational survival, scale expansion, and competitive victory; individual interests, and even life and death, can be sacrificed for group goals**.

The screening object of natural selection is never a single meme or a single individual, but the entire meme carrier group.

### Rule Two: Group-Level Collaborative Replication

The core replication goal of meme groups is not to control individual persons, but to expand the scale of the carrier group, achieving cross-regional, cross-generational propagation of the entire meme pool.

**Essence**: The entire cross-individual meme group, through the hands of countless individuals, completes group-level self-purification, ensuring the unity of group consensus.

**Case**: In mainstream narratives, the "standard of success" has changed from "glorifying ancestors" to "getting promoted and rich" to "financial freedom"; individual memes keep changing, but the core meme group of "must pursue worldly success" remains stable.

**Essence**: Meme groups, by adjusting individual memes to adapt to the era's environment, preserve the scale and survival of the entire carrier group.
