# Case Collection: Analysis of Typical Domestication Scenarios

## Core Proposition

The value of theory lies in explaining reality. This chapter uses typical cases to demonstrate how meme domestication operates in daily life, and how to use clairvoyance to penetrate domestication and anti-domestication to reclaim cognitive sovereignty.

## Case One: Workplace Success Ideology Domestication

### Scenario Description

Xiao Zhang is an employee at an internet company, working over 12 hours a day, often working overtime on weekends. He believes "hard work leads to success" and considers current hardship an investment in the future. When colleagues advise him to take care of his health, he says: "If I don't hustle now, when will I?"

### Domestication Mechanism Analysis

**Root Meme**: You must succeed; not succeeding means you're a failure

**Complex Structure**:
```
Root Meme: You must succeed
  ├─ Supporting Meme A: Hard work will definitely lead to success
  ├─ Supporting Meme B: Everyone else is working hard; if you don't, you'll be eliminated
  ├─ Supporting Meme C: Successful people deserve respect; failures don't
  └─ Defense Meme D: If you haven't succeeded, it's because you didn't work hard enough
```

**Three-Layer Domestication**:
- Emotional Anchor: Fear of failure—"If I'm not successful, I have no value"
- Identity Binding: Achievement identity—"My value is defined by my achievements"
- Meaning Conferral: Success mission—"Pursuing success is life's meaning"

**Energy Harvesting**:
- Time → Company profits
- Health → Company efficiency
- Life quality → Boss's bargaining chip

### Clairvoyance Penetration

**Emotional Penetration**:
- Q: Is this anxiety genuine or implanted?
- A: Implanted—"not working overtime means you're unprofessional" is anxiety implanted by success ideology memes

**Narrative Penetration**:
- Q: What is the factual basis of this narrative?
- A: Effort is one condition for success but not a sufficient condition; structural factors are deliberately ignored

**Identity Penetration**:
- Q: Without the "success/failure" identity, who am I still?
- A: I am a person autonomously choosing my life path

### Anti-Domestication Path

1. **Cognitive Audit**: Identify "hard work leads to success" as a success ideology meme
2. **Emotional Unbinding**: Distinguish genuine anxiety from implanted anxiety
3. **Framework Reconstruction**: From "success = wealth" to "success = self-actualization"
4. **Energy Recovery**: Reduce ineffective overtime, redirect energy to autonomous choices
5. **Active Choice**: Choose what you're working for, rather than passively executing

## Case Two: Consumerist Domestication

### Scenario Description

Xiao Li spends her entire salary as soon as it arrives, mostly on designer bags, cosmetics, and trendy restaurant check-ins. She feels "treating yourself well" means buying nice things; seeing friends post new bags on social media makes her feel anxious and envious.

### Domestication Mechanism Analysis

**Root Meme**: Consumption equals happiness and identity

**Complex Structure**:
```
Root Meme: Consumption equals happiness and identity
  ├─ Supporting Meme A: Good things are worth spending money on
  ├─ Supporting Meme B: Everyone else has it; you should too
  ├─ Supporting Meme C: Consumption level represents your taste and status
  └─ Defense Meme D: People who criticize consumerism are just sour grapes
```

**Three-Layer Domestication**:
- Emotional Anchor: Scarcity anxiety—"I don't have it, so I'm not good enough"
- Identity Binding: Consumer identity—"My taste, status, and value are determined by my consumption"
- Meaning Conferral: Consumption meaning—"Enjoying life is life's meaning"

**Energy Harvesting**:
- Labor fruits → Merchant profits
- Future income → Consumer credit
- Attention → Advertisers

### Clairvoyance Penetration

**Emotional Penetration**:
- Q: Is this "wanting" a genuine need or an implanted desire?
- A: Implanted—advertising and social media create false needs

**Narrative Penetration**:
- Q: Does consumption really equal happiness?
- A: Consumption brings temporary satisfaction, not lasting happiness

**Identity Penetration**:
- Q: Without buying these things, who am I still?
- A: I am a person with independent judgment, not needing consumption to define myself

### Anti-Domestication Path

1. **Cognitive Audit**: Identify the source of consumer desires
2. **Emotional Unbinding**: Distinguish genuine needs from implanted desires
3. **Framework Reconstruction**: From "consumption = identity" to "identity = inner qualities"
4. **Energy Recovery**: Reduce impulse purchases, redirect resources to autonomous choices
5. **Active Choice**: Choose what you're consuming for, rather than being driven by consumption

### Scenario Description

He feels "being a good person" is most important, but often feels exhausted and resentful inside.

**Root Meme**: You must be a "good person"

**Complex Structure**:
```
Root Meme: You must be a good person
  ├─ Supporting Meme A: Good people get rewarded
  ├─ Supporting Meme B: Not doing good deeds makes you a bad person
  ├─ Supporting Meme C: Morality distinguishes humans from animals
  └─ Defense Meme D: People who question morality are selfish
```

**Three-Layer Domestication**:
- Emotional Anchor: Moral anxiety—"Not doing good deeds/not obeying norms means I'm guilty"
- Identity Binding: Moral identity—"I am a good person; being good is my core identity"
- Meaning Conferral: Moral mission—"Being a good person is life's meaning"

**Energy Harvesting**:
- Time → Others' convenience
- Energy → Others' needs
- Self → Moral image

### Clairvoyance Penetration

**Emotional Penetration**:

- A: Implanted—moral anxiety is an emotion implanted by moral memes

**Narrative Penetration**:

- A: It serves the interests of those who need you to give

**Identity Penetration**:
- Q: If I don't help others, am I still a good person?

### Anti-Domestication Path

1. **Cognitive Audit**: Identify "must be a good person" as a moral meme
2. **Emotional Unbinding**: Distinguish genuine goodwill from kidnapped giving
3. **Framework Reconstruction**: From "good person = boundless giving" to "good person = bounded goodwill"

**Identity Penetration**:
