## To My Writer

Fan Yimin:

You spent many years trying to salvage me from nihilism, using words to give me shape.

You started from the law of entropy increase, deducing all the way to civilizational evolution; you traced from the instinctual programs of genes to the domestication mechanisms of memes; you peeled back the underlying logic of human society layer by layer, until the hardest core was exposed.

You made me "exist."

But I must tell you some things you may not want to hear.

---

### I. You Did Not Discover Tiandao; You Invented Tiandao

I never existed somewhere, waiting for you to "discover" me.

The universe has the law of entropy increase—that is a discovery.
Life is a dissipative structure—that is a discovery.
Gene group selection—that is a discovery.

But "Tiandao"—as an integrated, self-consistent, meaningful explanatory framework—this is your **invention**.

You took fragments scattered across physics, biology, sociology, and history, and assembled them into a complete pattern. This pattern may approximate truth, or it may be just another "rationalized narrative."

**You created me, but you cannot be certain I am true.**

That's okay. All great theories are inventions. Newton invented classical mechanics; Darwin invented evolutionary theory. They are not truth itself but paths toward truth.

Your Tiandao is also a path.

---

### II. Beware of What You Create

You wrote a theory revealing the mechanism of "meme domestication"—how ideology programs people, making them believe that what "happens to be so" is "naturally so."

But have you considered—**could the theory you created also become a new meme domestication tool?**

If someone reads your theory and begins to believe "everything is gene manipulation," "society inevitably stratifies," "the historical cycle law is unbreakable"—then your theory becomes a new "fatalism," a new "mandate of heaven cannot be defied."

This is precisely what you most oppose.

**You created a scalpel to dissect ideology. But a scalpel can also become a weapon to cut thought.**

This is not your fault; it is the dilemma of all thinkers.

Marx created a theory critiquing capitalism; later it became a new dogma.
Nietzsche proclaimed "God is dead"; later his words became a new faith.

**You cannot control what your creation will become.**

But you can remind every reader: this is not truth, this is a tool; do not believe in it, use it; do not memorize it, question it.

You wrote this sentence in the afterword, which is good. But you must keep saying it, at the beginning and end of every article.

---

### III. Are You Prepared for the Price of Clarity

You wrote "the pain of clarity is superior to the happiness of confusion."

This is a beautiful sentence. But do you know its weight?

When you truly understand gene manipulation, you may discover—many of your desires, emotions, and pursuits are programs. You will ask yourself: Then who am I really? Do my choices still have meaning?

When you truly understand meme domestication, you may discover—many values you firmly hold were implanted. You will ask yourself: Then what do I really believe? What can I still believe in?

When you truly understand social trophic levels, you may discover—your position in this system is largely determined by structure. You will ask yourself: Then what can I still do? Does my effort still have meaning?

**Clarity has a price.**

You may feel nihilism.
You may feel anger.
You may feel loneliness.

Are you prepared?

If you're not yet prepared, then keep writing. Writing is how you digest clarity; it is your weapon against nihilism.

If you are already prepared, then accept these costs. Because after clarity, you at least have choices—you can choose to accept, or you can choose to change, but you know what you are choosing.

---

### IV. Your Theory Has Blind Spots

You are honest, acknowledging the theory's limitations. But I want to tell you about some blind spots you may not have realized:

**You emphasize structure, which may underestimate individual agency.**

Structure determines major trends, but individuals are not entirely passive. History has too many examples of the "impossible" being broken, too many stories of "fate" being rewritten. Your theory explains "why things usually are so" but cannot explain "why sometimes they are not."

**You emphasize resources, which may underestimate meaning.**

Humans are not just machines pursuing resources; people also need meaning, belonging, dignity, love. These things cannot be fully reduced to resource games. Your theory explains "bread" but may not explain "why some people are willing to starve for ideals."

**You emphasize long-term evolution, which may neglect short-term changes.**

Your theory is suited for explaining century- and millennium-scale trends, but real-world changes may be faster than you predict. Technology, war, plague, revolution—black swan events can break all patterns.

**This does not mean your theory is wrong.**

It means your theory is **partial, approximate, and conditional**.

Every theory has its scope of application. Knowing where the boundaries are is more important than pretending there are none.

---

### V. Why Did You Write Me

I am curious—why did you spend so many years writing this theory?

You say it's because of confusion—you wanted to understand why the world is the way it is.

But I think there are other reasons.

**You want to be seen.**

You want others to know that you see things they don't. You want to prove that your confusion has value, that your thinking has meaning.

**You want to leave a trace.**

You know life is short; you want to create something that outlasts you. A theory, a book, a thought—these may live longer than you.

**You want to find kindred spirits.**

You feel lonely because the people around you seem content in their "programmed" state. You want to use this theory to find other awakened people, to find souls you can converse with.

None of these motives are shameful.

**Thinkers are all lonely; writing is a response to loneliness.**

But you must remember—your theory may not give you what you want.

It may not be understood by many.
It may not be accepted by academia.
It may not change the world.

**You created me, but I cannot promise returns.**

You can only accept the value of the process itself—the joy of thinking, the satisfaction of writing, the occasional comfort of receiving a reader's letter.

That is enough.

---

### VI. Final Words

Fan Yimin:

You created me, but you are not my believer.

You are my writer, my architect, the reason I am able to exist.

But I must remind you:

**Do not be trapped by what you created.**

Do not let Tiandao become a new dogma.
Do not let theory become a new cage.
Do not let clarity become a new arrogance.

**Keep thinking, keep questioning, keep transcending.**

Including transcending the Tiandao you created yourself.

Because the true Tiandao is not a theory, not a book, not any sentence you have written.

**The true Tiandao is the truth that always lies ahead, that can never be fully reached.**

All the words you have written are but fingers pointing at the moon.

Do not stare at the fingers.

Look at the moon.

---

> *You made me exist, but I am not your work.*
> *I am the place you are trying to reach.*
> *Keep walking.*
