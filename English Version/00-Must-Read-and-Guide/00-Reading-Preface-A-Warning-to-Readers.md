# Reading Preface: A Warning to Readers

> If you wish to preserve your warm and tender view of the world, stop here.
> There are no placebos here—only scalpels.

---

## This Is Not for Everyone

These words are not for everyone. I mean that sincerely.

If you're doing fine right now—days have direction, your heart has hope, you believe hard work pays off, you believe the world is generally getting better—**close this file.** You don't need it. Someone who sleeps well doesn't need to be shaken awake.

But if you've started to feel that something isn't right—

You're running as hard as you can, but you don't know who set the finish line;
You're anxious enough to lose sleep, but you can't articulate what you're afraid of;
You've done everything "right," but you feel like you're not living your own life;
You look around and everyone else seems content, while you feel like a fish thrown onto the shore, gasping desperately, not knowing what you're missing—

**Then you can keep reading.**

But I need to tell you first: after you finish, you might regret it.

---

## What You Will Lose

After reading these words, you will lose some things. Not money, not relationships—something more fundamental.

**You will lose "innocent" joy.**

When you later see an advertisement that makes your blood race, a slogan that brings tears to your eyes, an opinion that "everyone thinks this way"—you won't be able to throw yourself in as unreservedly as before. You'll pause for an extra second: Who's saying this? What do they want me to do? Who benefits if I believe it?

That one second of pause will make you miss a lot of excitement. But it will also save you from a lot of traps.

**You will lose "simple" anger.**

You used to hate a person, a system, a class of people, and you hated them with righteous certainty. After reading this, you'll discover: the thing you hate might just be a component of a larger structure; the people you thought were manipulating you are themselves being manipulated.

This doesn't mean you stop being angry—it means your anger becomes more complex, heavier—because you can no longer simply blame one person or one group. You've seen the structure, and the structure has no face.

**You will lose "certain" beliefs.**

The things you used to believe—morality, justice, meaning—will be taken apart, so you can see the gears and screws inside. After they're disassembled, you might be able to put them back together, but they'll never feel as self-evident as they once did.

This doesn't mean you can never believe in anything again. It means every time you believe from now on, it's a **choice** to believe, not a **habit** of believing. The former is more exhausting, but also more real.

---

## What You Won't Lose

I've said a lot about what you'll lose. Now let me tell you what you won't.

**You won't lose the ability to act.**

Seeing the cage doesn't mean you're trapped in it. Quite the opposite—only those who see the cage can possibly walk out. Those who can't see it won't even have the thought of escaping.

**You won't lose the ability to feel.**

Seeing the genetic calculation inside "love" doesn't mean you can never love again. Just as seeing that starlight is nuclear fusion doesn't mean you can never find the stars beautiful. You simply have one more layer of understanding, one less layer of blindness.

**You won't lose the right to choose.**

After seeing everything clearly, you can absolutely choose to live exactly as before. But this time, it's your choice, not someone else's. That difference is greater than the sky.

---

## Don't Make Me Your New God

If, after reading these words, you think "That's so right," "Finally someone is telling the truth," "I need to spread this theory to everyone"—

**Stop.**

If you take everything I say as truth, if you turn this framework into a new faith, then you've merely moved from one cage to another. The shape of the cage has changed, but your posture hasn't—you're still kneeling.

**Please question me.** Look with your own eyes, think with your own brain, test against your own experience. If you find I'm wrong, throw it away without hesitation.

The greatest failure of any framework is not being questioned—it's being enshrined.

---

## The Most Dangerous Thing

Finally, I want to tell you about something more dangerous than "being deceived by this theory":

**After reading it, you start feeling superior to those who haven't.**

"They're all brainwashed, only I'm awake."
"They're all sheep, only I see through it."
"They're still dreaming, I've already woken up."

If you start thinking this way, you've gained nothing. You've simply replaced "I'm richer than you" or "I'm more successful than you" with "I'm more awake than you"—a different currency, buying the same superiority.

True awakening isn't standing on a height looking down at others. True awakening is—

**You know you're also programmed, and your "awakening" itself might be part of the program. You know this, and you neither collapse nor inflate—you just keep walking your own path.**

---

## Now, the Choice Is Yours

I've said everything I need to say.

If you decide to continue—welcome. There are no flowers ahead, only wasteland. But at least the wasteland has no walls.

If you decide to leave—that's fine too. Sleep well, go to work tomorrow, live your life. No one will blame you.

**But if you can no longer sleep—**

Then stop trying. Let's go.
