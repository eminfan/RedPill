# 6 Symbiotic Tiandao Practice

## 15.1 Core Proposition: Fundamental Rupture with Predatory Tiandao

### The Historical Fate of Predatory Tiandao

All civilizations in human history, without exception, have fallen into the cycle of predatory Tiandao:

| Stage | Characteristics | Inevitable Outcome |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| Establishment | Breaking old order, relatively fair resources | New predatory structure sprouts |
| Stability | Predation mechanisms institutionalized, class solidification | Bottom-layer energy continuously drains |
| Collapse | Excessive predation, system imbalance | Peasant uprisings, foreign invasions, dynasty replacement |

**The Essence of Predatory Tiandao**: Not a moral problem of any individual or class, but the **endogenous inevitability of predatory structure**—as long as institutionalized uncompensated appropriation exists, the system will inevitably move toward collapse.

### The Fundamental Difference of Symbiotic Tiandao

**Symbiotic Tiandao is not an "improved version" of predatory Tiandao, but a fundamental rupture with predatory Tiandao.**

| Dimension | Predatory Tiandao | Symbiotic Tiandao |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Core Logic | Predatory profit-seeking → Resources concentrate upward | Symbiotic value-addition → Resources flow bidirectionally |
| Structural Form | Pyramid (unidirectional bloodsucking) | Network (bidirectional nourishment) |
| Class Relations | Solidified (intergenerational monopoly) | Mobile (capability-oriented) |
| System Fate | Cyclical collapse | Sustained evolution |
| Nature of Power | Predation tool (unconstrained) | Public service (constrained) |

### The Core Question of Practice

**What efforts different from the past are needed to achieve symbiotic Tiandao?**

This is not a question of "how to do better" but a question of "how to do something fundamentally different."

## 15.2 Effort One: Abolishing Institutionalized Predation—Fundamental Rupture with the Past

### Past Approach: Changing People, Not the System

All "revolutions" and "reforms" in history were essentially **changing who does the predating**:

| Historical Event | Surface Change | Unchanged Essence |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Peasant uprisings | Overthrowing old dynasty | New dynasty continues predation |
| Dynasty changes | Replacing rulers | Predatory structure continues |
| Reform movements | Adjusting policies | Institutionalized predation retained |

**Root Cause**: Not touching the institutional foundation of predation—private ownership of means of production and unconstrained power.

### New China's Different Effort: Changing the System, Not the People

**Core Rupture**: Not replacing one group of rulers with another, but abolishing the institutional foundation of rulers.

#### 1. Land System Revolution

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Land reform allows buying and selling, leading to consolidation | Public land ownership, institutionally blocking consolidation |
| Peasants gain land but may lose it again | Peasants permanently hold land use rights |
| Solving immediate problems, not root causes | Institutionally eliminating the foundation of land predation |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Land is the most core means of production in agricultural society; land consolidation is the root cause of predatory Tiandao's cyclical collapse. Public land ownership institutionally **cuts off the material foundation of predation**.

#### 2. Public Ownership of Means of Production

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Protecting private property as sacred | Public ownership of means of production, eliminating the foundation of exploitation |
| Capital hires labor, extracting surplus value | Workers collectively own the means of production |
| Wealth polarization is a "natural law" | Institutionally eliminating the foundation of polarization |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Private ownership of means of production is the institutional foundation of capital's predation on labor. Public ownership institutionally **cuts off the foundation of capital predation**.

#### 3. Abolishing Hierarchical Systems

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Laws recognizing hierarchical differences | Equality before the law |
| Nobility enjoying privileges | Abolishing all privileged classes |
| Identity determining destiny | Identity no longer solidified |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Hierarchical systems are the institutional foundation of class solidification. Abolishing hierarchical systems institutionally **cuts off the foundation of identity-based predation**.

## 15.3 Effort Two: Building Power Constraint Mechanisms—Fundamental Rupture with the Past

### Past Approach: Unconstrained Power

Under predatory Tiandao, power is a tool of predation:

| Manifestation | Consequence |
|--------------|-------------|
| Hereditary power | Intergenerational monopoly, class solidification |
| Power not constrained by law | Predation by power legalized |
| Lack of oversight mechanisms | Corruption institutionalized |
| Power-capital alliance | Power-wealth integration |

### New China's Different Effort: Caging Power

#### 1. Rule of Law Above Rule of Man

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| The emperor's word is law | Constitutional supremacy, law regulates power |
| Power can arbitrarily interpret law | Power must operate within the legal framework |
| "Princes who break the law are punished like commoners" is just a slogan | Establishing a judicial system, implementing legal constraints |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Rule of law is a rigid mechanism for constraining power, preventing power from alienating into a predatory tool.

#### 2. Comprehensive Oversight System

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Only superiors overseeing subordinates | Party oversight, NPC oversight, democratic oversight, public opinion oversight |
| Oversight is "self-oversight" | External oversight combined with internal oversight |
| Oversight is a mere formality | Oversight has institutional guarantees |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Oversight is the key mechanism for preventing power alienation; unchecked power inevitably corrupts.

#### 3. Self-Revolution Mechanism

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Ruling group's interests solidify | Party's self-purification, self-improvement |
| Internal reform faces enormous resistance | Self-revolution breaks interest solidification |
| Reforms often fail | Self-revolution becomes institutionalized |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Self-revolution is the core vitality of symbiotic Tiandao, the second answer to breaking out of the historical cycle law.

## 15.4 Effort Three: Reconstructing Resource Distribution Logic—Fundamental Rupture with the Past

### Past Approach: Resources Concentrate Upward

Under predatory Tiandao, resource flow is unidirectional:

```
Production Layer (creates value) → Transmission Layer (intercepts portion) → Enrichment Layer (concentrates surplus)
        ↑                                      ↓
        └──────────── Deprived ────────────────┘
```

**Result**: The bottom layer gets poorer, the top layer gets richer, the system inevitably collapses.

### New China's Different Effort: Bidirectional Resource Flow

#### 1. Public Finance Directed Toward All People

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Finance serves the ruling class | Finance serves all people |
| Taxes used to maintain ruling order | Taxes used for education, healthcare, infrastructure |
| Bottom layer lacks public services | Public services cover all people |

**Specific Practice**:

| Field | Investment | Effect |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Education | Nine-year compulsory education, massification of higher education | Literacy rate from 20% to over 95% |
| Healthcare | Basic medical insurance, public health system | Life expectancy from 35 to 78 years |
| Infrastructure | Transportation, communications, energy networks | Infrastructure covering the entire country |
| Poverty Alleviation | Targeted poverty alleviation, poverty eradication | Nearly 100 million people lifted out of poverty in 8 years |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Resources change from unidirectional predation to bidirectional flow, realizing the "Group Advantage Theorem"—incremental resources directed toward all people, enhancing overall vitality.

#### 2. Social Security Safety Net

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Bottom layer responsible for own survival | Social security ensures survival |
| Famine years with widespread starvation | Disaster relief system |
| No support in old age | Pension insurance system |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Social security is the institutional barrier preventing bottom-layer survival crises, key to breaking out of the historical cycle law.

#### 3. Common Prosperity Goal

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Wealth polarization is "natural law" | Common prosperity is the essence of socialism |
| Polarization is unavoidable | Narrowing gaps through institutional regulation |
| Efficiency first, fairness later | Dynamic balance of efficiency and fairness |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Common prosperity is the core goal of symbiotic Tiandao, the practical embodiment of the Anti-Predation Theorem.

## 15.5 Effort Four: Establishing Fair Mobility Channels—Fundamental Rupture with the Past

### Past Approach: Placebo Channels

Under predatory Tiandao, class mobility channels are essentially **placebos**:

| Channel | Surface Function | Actual Function |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Imperial examinations | Providing upward opportunities | Siphoning off elites, pacifying the bottom layer |
| Military merit | Commoners can be ennobled | Dying for the ruling class |
| Commerce | Business can create wealth | Wealth without political status |

**Essence**: Channels exist, but mobility rates are extremely low, and channels can close at any time.

### New China's Different Effort: Genuine Mobility Channels

#### 1. Education Equity

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Education is aristocratic privilege | Nine-year compulsory education, everyone can attend school |
| College entrance exam is a placebo | College entrance exam is a genuine fair channel |
| Unequal educational resources | Continuously advancing education equity |

**Data**:
- Since reform and opening up, hundreds of millions have achieved class mobility through education
- Middle-income group grew from nearly zero to 400 million

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Education equity is the core practice of the Anti-Solidification Theorem, a genuine mobility channel.

#### 2. Market Opportunities

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Commerce discriminated against | Market economy, entrepreneurial freedom |
| Wealth unprotected | Protecting legitimate property rights |
| Only one path within the system | Multiple development paths |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: The market economy provides multiple mobility paths, activating individual vitality.

#### 3. Capability Orientation

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Identity determines destiny | Capability determines development |
| Intergenerational monopoly | Intergenerational mobility |
| Birth determines everything | Effort can change destiny |

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Capability orientation is the core of the Anti-Solidification Theorem, the fundamental difference between symbiotic Tiandao and predatory Tiandao.

|--------------|-------------|

| Suppressing criticism | Losing opportunities for correction |

| Past Approach | New China's Difference |

| Maintaining a "correct image" | Seeking truth from facts, correcting errors when found |

**Cases**:

**Tiandao Theory Significance**: Theoretical iteration is the practice of the Dynamic Iteration Theorem, the core vitality of symbiotic Tiandao.

#### 3. Self-Revolution

## 15.8 Conclusion: Historical Significance of Symbiotic Tiandao Practice

### Fundamental Differences from the Past

| Dimension | Past Approach | New China's Different Effort |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------------|
| Predatory relations | Changing who preys | Abolishing institutionalized predation |
| Nature of power | Predation tool, unconstrained | Public service, constrained |
| Resource flow | Unidirectional upward concentration | Bidirectional flow, shared by all |
| Class mobility | Placebo channels, mainly solidified | Genuine channels, capability-oriented |
| Error-correction mechanism | Refusing to acknowledge until collapse | Self-revolution, continuous correction |
| External relations | Dependency or predation | Independence and self-reliance |

### Core Conclusion

**The practice of symbiotic Tiandao is not an improvement within the framework of predatory Tiandao, but a fundamental rupture with predatory Tiandao.**

This rupture is manifested in:

1. **Institutional Rupture**: Abolishing the material foundation of institutionalized predation (public land ownership, public ownership of means of production)
2. **Power Rupture**: Caging power, preventing power from alienating into a predatory tool
3. **Distribution Rupture**: Resources changing from unidirectional predation to bidirectional flow
4. **Mobility Rupture**: From placebo channels to genuine fair channels
5. **Mechanism Rupture**: From refusing error-correction to self-revolution
6. **Relational Rupture**: From dependency and predation to independence and self-reliance

### Historical Significance

> New China's symbiotic Tiandao practice is humanity's first attempt to fundamentally break the cyclical fate of predatory Tiandao. This practice proves: symbiotic Tiandao is not a utopia but a realistic path achievable through institutional innovation, power constraints, resource reconstruction, mobility opening, self-correction, and independence.

**Final Judgment**:

> The success of symbiotic Tiandao practice does not lie in building a perfect system at once, but in consistently adhering to the core goal of "maximizing the group's long-term survival and evolutionary advantage," possessing endogenous self-correction and self-revolution mechanisms, and being able to continuously correct deviations, continuously iterate, and spiral upward in practice. This is the fundamental difference from all past efforts.
