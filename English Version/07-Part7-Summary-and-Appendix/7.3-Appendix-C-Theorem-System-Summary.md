# Appendix C: Theorem System Summary

## I. Predatory Tiandao Theorems

### Theorem 1: Pyramid Structure Theorem
**Spontaneous order inevitably forms a pyramid stratification of "many bottom-layer producers, few top-layer controllers," with resources concentrating upward from below.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Surplus Resource Natural Enrichment Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Social stratification presents a conical structure

### Theorem 2: Class Solidification Theorem
**The top layer converts power, resources, and advantages into intergenerational transmission; class mobility opportunities continuously decrease over time, and barriers become increasingly rigid.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Rational Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Intergenerational transmission of educational, economic, and social capital

### Theorem 3: Placebo Channel Theorem
**The system retains extremely low-probability upward channels, aimed at pacifying the bottom layer and suppressing resistance, rather than truly breaking stratification.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism
- **Manifestation**: Selection systems, success myths, consumerism

### Theorem 4: Moral Instrument Theorem
**The core function of lower-order morality is to domesticate the bottom layer into accepting the status quo and weakening resistance, serving the stability of the predatory system.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Legitimization Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Packaging obedience as virtue, treating resistance as evil

### Theorem 5: Technology Efficiency Enhancement Theorem
**Incremental gains created by technology are monopolized and plundered by the top layer first, only improving the scale and concealment of predation without changing its essence.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem
- **Manifestation**: Technological progress exacerbates wealth polarization

### Theorem 6: Endogenous Cycle Theorem
**Internal predation continuously intensifies → resource distribution becomes severely imbalanced → bottom-layer survival comes under pressure → social collapse and reconstruction.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dissipative Structure Survival Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Dynasty rise-and-fall cycles

## II. Symbiotic Tiandao Theorems

### Theorem 7: Compliance Theorem
**Symbiotic Tiandao does not negate survival needs, resource limitations, group evolution, or other foundational laws; it only reconstructs resource distribution and power rules.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meta-axiom system
- **Core Logic**: Achieving better resource distribution through institutional design while respecting objective laws

### Theorem 8: Anti-Predation Theorem
**Abolish through institutions "uncompensated appropriation based on monopoly of means of production/power"; there is no institutionalized predation within humanity, only equivalent exchange and reasonable division-of-labor returns.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Predatory Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Curbing predatory behavior through institutional design, ensuring fair resource distribution

### Theorem 9: Group Advantage Theorem
**Incremental resources are directed toward universal education, healthcare, innovation, infrastructure, and security; overall vitality, risk resistance, and long-term survival quality far exceed lower-order systems.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaborative Value-Addition Axiom
- **Core Logic**: Enhancing the group's overall quality and capability through public investment

### Theorem 10: Anti-Solidification Theorem
**Class mobility is based solely on individual capability, labor, and contribution, blocking intergenerational monopoly advantages and maintaining stable mobility.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse design of the Class Solidification Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Establishing fair competition mechanisms, ensuring equal opportunity

### Theorem 11: Cycle-Breaking Theorem
**Eliminating the endogenous collapse driver of "predation infinitely escalating," resource distribution becomes sustainable, no longer falling into the cycle of predatory Tiandao.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Reverse design of the Endogenous Cycle Theorem
- **Core Logic**: Achieving balanced energy flow through institutional design

## III. Social Trophic Level Theory Theorems

### Theorem 12: Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem
**In the spontaneous state without external rigid constraints, surplus resources inevitably flow continuously from the production layer to the enrichment layer; the enrichment layer's wealth share monotonically increases over time.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Surplus Resource Natural Enrichment Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Wealth gap continuously widening

### Theorem 13: Structural Stability Theorem
**The stability cycle of predation-dominated structures is strictly negatively correlated with predation intensity and strictly positively correlated with the production layer's survival guarantee level.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dissipative Structure Survival Axiom
- **Manifestation**: The greater the predation intensity, the more unstable the structure

### Theorem 14: Transmission Layer Alienation Theorem
**Without rigid institutional constraints, the transmission layer inevitably gradually forms a predatory interest alliance with the enrichment layer, abandoning its core functions of resource regulation and serving production.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Rational Profit-Seeking Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Bureaucratic corruption, institutional failure

### Theorem 15: Efficiency Limit Theorem
**The resource utilization efficiency of predation-dominated structures has a rigid upper limit; efficiency improvement can only be achieved by increasing predation intensity on the production layer or squeezing the transmission layer's reasonable returns.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Unidirectional Resource Flow Theorem
- **Manifestation**: Sluggish economic growth, intensifying social contradictions

### Theorem 16: Dead Steady State Theorem
**When a predation-dominated structure simultaneously satisfies three conditions—"weak predation intensity (not breaking through the production layer's survival baseline), complete class solidification (no upward mobility space), and strong ideological domestication (dissolving resistance will)"—it forms a low-level dead steady state.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism
- **Manifestation**: Society lacks vitality, innovation stagnates

### Theorem 17: Symbiotic Efficiency Theorem
**The resource utilization efficiency of symbiosis-dominated structures has no rigid upper limit and can achieve long-term exponential growth through the release of universal innovation vitality, continuous improvement of collaboration efficiency, and breakthroughs in technological progress.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaborative Value-Addition Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Sustained economic development, society full of vitality

### Theorem 18: Long-Term Evolutionary Competition Theorem
**In cross-group, cross-civilizational long-term evolutionary competition, the long-term survival probability, risk resistance, and development potential of symbiosis-dominated structures are significantly higher than those of predation-dominated structures.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Group Evolution Priority Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Symbiotic civilizations are more competitive

### Theorem 19: Dynamic Balance Theorem
**Symbiosis-dominated structures achieve dynamic balance between efficiency and fairness through institutional design, maintaining both individual innovation motivation and social fairness and justice.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Collaborative Value-Addition Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Unity of efficiency and fairness

### Theorem 20: Self-Optimization Theorem
**Symbiosis-dominated structures possess self-optimization capability, able to continuously adapt to environmental changes through institutional innovation and cultural evolution, achieving sustained development.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dual Inheritance System Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Continuous innovation in institutions and culture

## IV. Gene Group Evolution Theory Theorems

### Theorem 21: Dual Encoding Synergy Theorem
**The co-evolution of genes and memes is the core driving force of human social evolution; the two interact with and shape each other.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Dual Encoding Essence Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Genes provide physiological foundation, memes shape social behavior

### Theorem 22: Meme Domestication Effectiveness Theorem
**Meme domestication is an effective mechanism for social systems to maintain order, achieving coordination of individual behavior through non-violent means.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Meme Domestication Mechanism Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Internalization of social norms, conscious coordination of individual behavior

### Theorem 23: Cognitive Breakthrough Theorem
**The cognitive layer is the highest dimension of evolution; through dimension-reduction observation, it achieves consciousness's anti-domestication and cognitive sovereignty.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Negative Entropy Drive Logic Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Individual cognitive awakening, critical thinking about the system

### Theorem 24: Energy Transformation Theorem
**The stability of social systems depends on the effective transformation of individual energy into system energy; false subjectivity is the key mechanism for achieving this transformation.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Negative Entropy Drive Logic Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Individuals contributing energy under the illusion of "autonomous choice"

### Theorem 25: Group Selection Theorem
**Group selection is an important mechanism of biological evolution; characteristics favorable to group survival will be preserved and reinforced.**
- **Theoretical Basis**: Group Evolution Priority Axiom
- **Manifestation**: Evolution of altruistic behavior, formation of group cohesion
